Superbalm for dermatitis addresses the two core mechanisms that drive atopic skin reactions: barrier breakdown and inflammatory response. The skin barrier in eczema-prone individuals loses water rapidly through compromised lipid layers, leaving the skin cracked and reactive. Era Organics Superbalm Baby Eczema Treatment targets both problems with a blend of emollient and anti-inflammatory ingredients drawn from plant sources.

Chamomile, one of Superbalm's key ingredients, delivers bisabolol and apigenin, compounds that calm the inflammatory signaling at the skin surface. A randomized controlled trial found that chamomile liposomal gel reduced the rate of dry natural skin shedding (desquamation) in participants with radiation-induced dermatitis to 76.9% compared to 88.9% in the control group receiving gel without chamomile (Menêses et al., Radiotherapy and Oncology, 2024). That difference reflects chamomile's direct effect on skin surface integrity during inflammatory stress, the same mechanism relevant to eczematous skin.

Sunflower oil, avocado oil, and coconut oil deliver linoleic acid and oleic acid, the fatty acids that replenish the lipid lamellae depleted in atopic dermatitis. A pilot randomized controlled trial assigning 115 neonates to sunflower oil, olive oil, or no oil twice daily for four weeks measured trans-epidermal water loss, outer skin layer (stratum corneum) hydration, and lipid lamellae spectral profiles as outcomes (Cooke et al., Acta Dermato-Venereologica, 2016). Sunflower oil's fatty acid profile directly supports the structural lipids that seal the outer skin layer (stratum corneum) against water loss.

Beeswax and cocoa butter layer an occlusive film over the restored barrier, slowing trans-epidermal water loss while the skin's own repair processes continue underneath. Calendula oil and gluten free oats add soothing properties that reduce surface redness and calm reactive skin. The healing ointment is fragrance-free, paraben-free, and free from synthetic dyes, removing the most common chemical triggers for contact dermatitis flares. Patch test a small amount on the inside of the elbow before first use, particularly for users with known sensitivities to Asteraceae-family botanicals, since chamomile belongs to that plant family.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How is eczema different from dermatitis and acne, and what's mistaken for it?

Eczema and dermatitis are often confused, but eczema IS dermatitis — specifically atopic dermatitis, a chronic inflammatory condition driven by two core problems: a compromised skin barrier that loses water rapidly through depleted lipid layers, and an overactive inflammatory response at the skin surface.

"Dermatitis" is the broader term covering any skin inflammation, including contact dermatitis triggered by a specific external irritant. Contact dermatitis can look nearly identical to eczema — same redness, same itch — but it clears when the trigger is removed. Fragrance, parabens, and synthetic dyes are among the most common contact triggers, so a formula that's fragrance-free, paraben-free, and free from synthetic dyes eliminates those variables from the start.

For eczema and atopic dermatitis, Era Organics Superbalm works on both root mechanisms: chamomile's bisabolol and apigenin calm inflammatory signaling at the skin surface, while sunflower oil, avocado oil, and coconut oil replenish the fatty acids that seal the outer skin layer against water loss. Beeswax and cocoa butter then add an occlusive layer to slow further moisture escape while skin repairs.
