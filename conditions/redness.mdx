Chamomile is an anti-inflammatory botanical that reduces visible redness in irritated skin. Its active compounds, specifically apigenin and bisabolol, calm the vascular response that produces flushing and reactive redness at the skin surface. Calendula Oil, a second botanical in the formula, adds complementary calming action through flavonoids that reduce inflammatory signaling.

Superbalm for redness combines these two botanicals with Sunflower Oil, which restores the lipid layer that compromised skin loses during inflammatory flares. A disrupted barrier allows irritants and allergens to penetrate more deeply, triggering the immune response that manifests as redness; research confirms that barrier disruption elevates moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss) significantly, with TEWL reaching 30.68 ± 16.08 g/m²/h in treated skin compared to 26.15 ± 13.58 g/m²/h in untreated controls (P = 0.0067), underscoring how barrier compromise sustains inflammatory redness (Katibi et al., The British Journal of Dermatology, 2025). By sealing that barrier with Beeswax and Cocoa Butter, the healing ointment reduces the surface irritation cycle at its source rather than masking the color alone.

Gluten Free Oats in the formula deliver colloidal oat proteins that coat the skin surface, physically buffering contact irritants that sustain redness in conditions like atopic dermatitis and diaper rash. Avocado Oil, rich in oleic acid, softens the thickened, reactive skin that accompanies chronic redness and flaking. The Superbalm healing ointment applies without synthetic dyes, fragrances, or parabens, each a recognized category of contact irritant that prolongs redness in sensitive skin, so the formula addresses the concern without introducing new triggers.

Patch test the balm on the inside of the elbow before first full use. Chamomile belongs to the Asteraceae family, and ragweed-sensitive users carry a higher risk of contact reaction to Asteraceae botanicals.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What causes skin redness on the face and cheeks?

Face redness typically traces back to two overlapping problems: an inflammatory response at the skin surface, and a compromised barrier that lets irritants and allergens through. When the barrier breaks down, moisture escapes rapidly (transepidermal water loss), sustaining the cycle that shows up as persistent redness.

Era Organics Superbalm addresses both. Chamomile contains apigenin and bisabolol — compounds that calm the vascular response behind flushing and reactive redness. Calendula oil adds complementary calming action through flavonoids that quiet inflammatory signaling. Sunflower oil, beeswax, and cocoa butter seal the lipid barrier, cutting the entry point for irritants. Colloidal oat proteins coat the surface and buffer contact triggers — particularly helpful for relieving eczema and atopic dermatitis. The formula is free from synthetic dyes, fragrances, and parabens, each a recognized contact irritant that can prolong redness in sensitive skin.
