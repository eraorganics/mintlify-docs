Superbalm for itchy skin addresses the underlying barrier disruption that generates the itch-scratch cycle in dry and eczema-prone skin. Nerve endings in the outer skin (epidermis) become sensitized, producing the persistent itching associated with atopic dermatitis and contact dermatitis when the skin's outer layer loses moisture. The balm's combination of occlusive and emollient ingredients seals that compromised barrier, reducing moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss) (TEWL) and the nerve-signal cascade behind chronic itch.

Chamomile, one of Superbalm's key ingredients, contains the anti-inflammatory compound bisabolol, which calms reactive skin and reduces surface redness that accompanies itching. Calendula Oil delivers a dense supply of flavonoids that quiet irritation at the skin surface. Beeswax forms a physical occlusive layer that locks moisture in and keeps environmental irritants out, directly interrupting the dryness-to-itch loop. Gluten Free Oats (Avena sativa) have a long-documented role in relieving itch: colloidal oat preparations reduce TEWL and itch intensity in atopic skin, and the Superbalm formula draws on this emollient grain as a core active.

Sunflower Oil and Avocado Oil contribute linoleic acid and oleic acid respectively, fatty acids that replenish the intercellular lipid matrix eroded in dry and eczema-compromised skin. A functioning lipid matrix is the physical substrate that keeps itch mediators from reaching cutaneous nerve fibers. Coconut Oil reinforces this barrier action; a randomized clinical study found topical coconut oil application altered skin-microbiome assembly in preterm infants during the first three weeks of life, pointing to a biologically active effect on the skin surface beyond simple occlusion (Ghori N et al., International Journal of Molecular Sciences, 2023). Vegetable Glycerin draws atmospheric moisture into the outer skin layer (stratum corneum), maintaining the hydration level that keeps skin calm between applications.

Patch test Baby Superbalm on the inside of the elbow before first full use. Chamomile belongs to the Asteraceae (ragweed) plant family and is a possible contact allergen for ragweed-sensitive users.
