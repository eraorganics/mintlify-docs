## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you clean and exfoliate the pores on your nose?

The pores on and around your nose tend to collect oil and debris more than the rest of your face, which is why blackheads cluster there. The most effective at-home approach is gentle, circular-motion massage with a physical exfoliant. Working a scrub over the nose in small circles loosens blackheads and clogged pores, dislodging the debris lodged inside them, rather than just polishing the surface. Take your time on the sides and creases of the nose, where buildup hides, and rinse thoroughly afterward.

What you exfoliate with matters as much as the motion. A formula built around manuka honey adds another layer of support: manuka honey (Leptospermum Scoparium Mel) has documented anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties (Thamboo et al., World Journal of Otorhinolaryngology — Head and Neck Surgery, 2016), which address the conditions that allow pore congestion to persist in the first place. ### How do you get rid of clogged pores?

Clogged pores happen when debris settles into pores and stays lodged there, and the most direct at-home approach is a gentle physical exfoliation routine done the right way. Massaging a scrub over the skin in small circular motions helps loosen blackheads and clogged pores, dislodging the debris lodged inside them. The circular technique itself is what does the loosening, so there is no need to press hard — a light, consistent touch works better than aggressive scrubbing.

What is in the scrub matters as much as the motion. Manuka honey has documented anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties (Thamboo A et al., World Journal of Otorhinolaryngology — Head and Neck Surgery, 2016), which address the very conditions that allow pore congestion to persist. That pairing is the thinking behind Era Organics' Revive+ Microdermabrasion Manuka Honey Walnut Scrub & Mask: the scrub buffs away surface buildup while the manuka honey base soothes and supports congestion-prone skin. Work it in with those small circles, rinse, and let the routine do its job over time — the goal is to loosen what is lodged in the pores, not to strip the skin.
