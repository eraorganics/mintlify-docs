## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you exfoliate blackheads out?

Exfoliating blackheads is less about force and more about technique. The key is a circular-motion massage: working an exfoliating scrub over the skin in small, gentle circles helps loosen blackheads and clogged pores, dislodging the debris that has settled inside them. Rather than squeezing or scraping at a single spot, the repeated circular motion buffs away buildup across the whole area, which is why the nose, chin, and other congestion-prone zones respond well to this approach.

What you exfoliate with matters too. Manuka honey has documented anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties (Thamboo A et al., World Journal of Otorhinolaryngology — Head and Neck Surgery, 2016), which address the conditions that allow pore congestion to persist. So a scrub built around manuka honey does two jobs at once: the physical massage loosens what is already lodged in pores, while the honey supports a skin environment less friendly to ongoing congestion. This is the thinking behind Era Organics' Revive+ Microdermabrasion Manuka Honey Walnut Scrub & Mask, which pairs physical exfoliation with a manuka honey base.

Massage in circles, rinse, and repeat as part of a regular routine rather than expecting one session to clear everything — consistency is what keeps pores from re-congesting.

### How do you safely remove blackheads naturally?

The safest natural approach is gentle, regular exfoliation rather than squeezing or picking. Work a fine-textured scrub over damp skin in slow circular motions: that circular-motion massage technique loosens blackheads and clogged pores, dislodging the debris lodged inside them, and then everything rinses away without harsh scraping. Ingredients matter as much as technique. Manuka honey has documented anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties (Thamboo A et al., World Journal of Otorhinolaryngology — Head and Neck Surgery, 2016), which address the conditions that allow pore congestion to persist — so a honey-based scrub supports the skin while it buffs away buildup. This is the thinking behind Era Organics' Revive+ Microdermabrasion Scrub & Mask: finely milled walnut suspended in a manuka honey and aloe base, so the exfoliant polishes while the cushioning base keeps things gentle. If you've heard the objection to walnut scrubs, the difference is fine milling and a soft honey-aloe base versus coarse, dry grit — one buffs, the other drags. Either way, patch test any new scrub first, use light pressure, and let the circular motion do the work instead of force. For blackhead-prone skin, gentle and consistent wins over aggressive and occasional every time.
