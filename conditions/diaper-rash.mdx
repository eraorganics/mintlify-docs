Superbalm for diaper rash draws on Chamomile and Calendula Oil to calm the visible redness and irritation that accompany prolonged skin contact with moisture. Sunflower Oil strengthens skin barrier function in young children. Fischer et al. Superbalm's diaper rash formula pairs sunflower oil with Arrowroot Powder, which absorbs surface moisture and reduces the damp microenvironment that worsens diaper dermatitis.

The balm is paraben-free, fragrance-free, and mineral-oil-free, removing three common irritants from contact with the diaper area's already-sensitized skin. Patch test a small amount on the inside of the elbow before first use, particularly for infants with known sensitivities to Asteraceae-family botanicals such as chamomile.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is this a regular diaper rash or a yeast infection?

Regular diaper rash is driven by friction and prolonged wetness against the skin — if the rash responds to barrier care and frequent changes, that's a good sign it's standard dermatitis. If it persists beyond several days or worsens despite good hygiene, have a pediatrician rule out a yeast infection, which requires antifungal treatment rather than barrier care.

### What is the best way to treat a diaper rash?

Diaper rash thrives on moisture and friction, so the core strategy is a barrier that keeps wetness off irritated skin. Change diapers frequently, let the area air-dry before applying cream, and choose a formula that is fragrance-free and paraben-free — both are common irritants on baby skin.
