Superbalm for chapped lips works through a combination of occlusive and emollient ingredients that seal moisture into dry, cracked lip tissue. Beeswax forms a protective film over the lip surface, slowing moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss) (TEWL) while the underlying skin recovers. Cocoa Butter and Coconut Oil deliver fatty acids that soften the keratin layer, restoring flexibility to skin that has become tight or fissured.

Era Organics Superbalm healing ointment draws on Calendula Oil and Chamomile to calm the inflammation that accompanies severely chapped lips, where the barrier has broken down enough to produce redness and rawness. Avocado Oil contributes oleic acid, which integrates readily into the outer skin layer (stratum corneum) (the outermost skin layer), restoring lipid content to depleted tissue. Vegetable Glycerin draws water from the deeper dermis toward the surface, maintaining hydration beneath the occlusive seal that Beeswax provides.

Sunflower seed oil applied to compromised skin showed a measurable effect on barrier function in a controlled study comparing natural oils; the same research found that not all natural oils equally support barrier recovery, showing the relevance of oil selection for fragile skin (Danby et al., Pediatric Dermatology, 2013). Sunflower Oil's high linoleic acid content targets the lipid pathways most depleted in dry, cracked skin, making it a functionally distinct choice from heavier saturated oils. Superbalm pairs Sunflower Oil with Arrowroot Powder, which reduces tackiness and makes the balm comfortable to wear on the lips without a greasy residue.

Apply a dime-sized amount of the healing ointment to clean, dry lips as frequently as needed. Chamomile is a member of the Asteraceae (ragweed) botanical family; users with ragweed sensitivity should patch test the balm on the inside of the elbow before applying it to the lip area.

### What causes chapped lips?

Chapped lips develop when the lip surface loses moisture faster than the tissue can replenish it — a process called transepidermal water loss (TEWL). As that moisture escapes, the outer keratin layer loses lipid content, tightens, and eventually fissures. In more severe cases, where the barrier has broken down enough to produce redness and rawness, the underlying tissue becomes exposed and the skin struggles to recover on its own.

Addressing chapped lips means working on both fronts at once: sealing the surface to slow further moisture loss while replenishing the lipid content the keratin layer has lost. Vegetable Glycerin draws water upward from the deeper skin, and Calendula Oil and Chamomile help calm redness and irritation where the barrier has broken down.
