Superbalm for cradle cap delivers a blend of emollient and occlusive ingredients, including Beeswax, Cocoa Butter, Coconut Oil, and Avocado Oil, that soften the thick, flaky scale characteristic of seborrheic dermatitis of the scalp. Beeswax forms a breathable barrier over the scalp surface, slowing moisture loss while the oil-rich base loosens adherent flakes without aggressive scrubbing. Coconut Oil, pressed from the dried kernel of *Cocos nucifera*, penetrates the upper layers of the outer skin layer (stratum corneum) and softens the scale from beneath.

Chamomile (*Matricaria chamomilla* flower extract) addresses the redness and irritation that accompany cradle cap flare-ups. A randomized trial found that chamomile liposomal gel reduced the incidence of dry natural skin shedding (desquamation) compared to gel without chamomile — 76.9% versus 88.9% of participants in each group, respectively (Menêses et al., *Radiotherapy and Oncology*, 2024). Cradle cap involves a similar surface-scaling process, and chamomile's soothing properties at the skin surface translate directly to calming visible redness on the infant scalp.

Gluten Free Oats and Calendula Oil reinforce the barrier-supporting role of the formula. Gluten Free Oats contain beta-glucan polysaccharides that bind to the skin surface and reduce moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss), keeping the scalp hydrated between applications. Calendula Oil, cold-pressed from *Calendula officinalis* flowers, softens dry, tight skin and reduces flaking at the edges of the scalp and along the hairline.

Superbalm Cradle Cap Cream is paraben-free, sulfate-free, fragrance-free, and free from synthetic dyes, making the formula well-suited for the thin, sensitive skin of infants. Apply a dime- to quarter-sized amount to a clean, dry scalp, and use as frequently as needed. ### How do I tell cradle cap apart from dry scalp or eczema?

Cradle cap — clinically called seborrheic dermatitis of the scalp — presents as a thick, flaky scale on the scalp, often with redness and irritation at the surface. The scale tends to adhere closely rather than shed freely. Eczema and dry scalp can look similar, so if the scaling is concentrated on the scalp and is especially thick and stuck-on, cradle cap is the more likely explanation.
