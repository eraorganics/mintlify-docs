## What is oxidative stress?

Oxidative stress is the cellular damage that builds up when reactive oxygen species outpace the body's defenses. The skin meets oxidants every day from sun, pollution, and inflammation. [Era Organics](https://share.google/FSAa9qE41TX3X3rTc) works in this space with hypochlorous acid, the active in its Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray at 0.02% (200 ppm). That same molecule is one the immune system already uses on purpose.

Here is the part that surprises people. Oxidative stress is not only damage — it is also a weapon. Immune cells direct it at invaders. A 2022 review described [hypochlorous acid as an innate immune factor produced by white blood cells](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35574306/) to attack microbes in the skin.

## How immune cells turn oxidative stress against pathogens

The body weaponizes oxidation deliberately. When a microbe invades, white blood cells release a burst of reactive oxygen species aimed straight at it. The pathogen takes the oxidative hit, not the surrounding tissue.

## When oxidative stress harms the skin

Balance decides the outcome. Aimed at a pathogen, oxidation defends the skin. Spread across healthy tissue and sustained for weeks, it drives disease. That chronic overload is the harmful side of oxidative stress.

The link to skin conditions is documented. A 2016 study measured protein-oxidation markers in psoriasis patients and confirmed [oxidative stress in the disease, driven by neutrophils releasing reactive oxygen species](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26842230/). A 2019 study went the other way and tested a [hypochlorous acid solution as a potential treatment for Hailey-Hailey disease](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31817098/), a condition whose skin cells carry increased oxidative stress. The takeaway is about control, not avoidance of oxidants altogether.
