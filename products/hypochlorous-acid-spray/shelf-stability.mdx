## What is shelf stability?

Shelf stability is how long a product keeps its intended properties in storage. For a hypochlorous acid spray, it means the active is still doing its job when you reach the last spray. Because HOCl is reactive, shelf stability depends on both the formula and how it is stored. [Era Organics](https://share.google/PYHreJRH76XyfSUYb) builds Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray with this in mind.

The starting point is a stable active. A 2020 review noted that hypochlorous acid must be [stabilized through technology to work as a topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Without that, the product would weaken well before the bottle emptied.

## What shortens a product's shelf life

Reactive ingredients age a formula fastest. Every additive is a potential reaction that consumes the active. Heat, light, and air speed the process further.

A minimal formula ages more slowly. Era Organics' spray holds only [hypochlorous acid](/ingredients/hypochlorous-acid) and electrolyzed water at 0.02% (200 ppm), so there is little to drive breakdown. ## Storing for a longer life

Storage does the rest of the work. Keep the bottle closed, cool, and out of direct light. Those simple steps protect the active between uses.

## Frequently asked questions about shelf stability

### How long does a hypochlorous acid spray last?

It depends on the formula and storage; always check the label for the product's period-after-opening. A minimal, stabilized formula holds up better than one with many reactive ingredients. Keep it cool and out of light to protect the active.

### How do I know if it has expired?

Follow the date and period-after-opening on the packaging. Store it properly and use it within that window. A short ingredient list helps it stay consistent to the end.

### Does refrigeration help?

Cool, stable storage away from heat and light is generally kind to hypochlorous acid. Follow any storage note on the label. Avoid leaving it in a hot car or sunny window.
