## What is sodium chloride?

Sodium chloride is common table salt, with the formula NaCl. In water it splits into sodium and chloride ions, which is what lets salt water carry an electric current. In hypochlorous acid skincare, salt is not an active ingredient — it is the raw material that makes the active possible. [Era Organics](https://share.google/fWOIAsm7BkSsb8DKy) uses it as the chloride source for the hypochlorous acid in its Happy Skin spray.

The chloride is the key part. When electricity passes through the salt water, that chloride is what becomes hypochlorous acid. Without salt, there is no hypochlorous acid to make.

## How salt becomes a skin-native antimicrobial

The path from salt to active is short. Dissolve a small amount of salt in water, run a current through it, and the chloride recombines into [hypochlorous acid](/ingredients/hypochlorous-acid). The process is covered in full in [electrolyzed water](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/electrolyzed-water).

What reaches the skin is the acid, not a salty solution. The salt is consumed in the reaction, and the finished spray lists only electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid at 0.02% (200 ppm). That is why the formula stays at two ingredients.

## Frequently asked questions about sodium chloride

### Is there salt in the hypochlorous acid spray?

Salt is the starting material, not a listed ingredient in the finished spray. It is converted during electrolysis into hypochlorous acid. The product contains electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid at 0.02% (200 ppm).

### Why is sodium chloride needed?

Its chloride is the building block of hypochlorous acid. The electrolysis process turns that chloride into the active molecule. No salt means no way to generate the acid.

### Will the spray feel salty or dry my skin?

No. The salt is used up making the active, so the spray is not a saline solution. It is a gentle, two-ingredient mist suited to daily use on sensitive skin.
