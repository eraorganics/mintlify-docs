## What is sodium chloride?

Sodium chloride is a salt — the ionic compound made of sodium and chloride ions, with the formula NaCl. It is the same substance known as common salt or table salt, and chemists write it simply as NaCl. As a salt, it dissolves readily in water and splits into its two ions, which is what lets salt water carry an electric current. In hypochlorous acid skincare it is not an active ingredient; it is the raw material that makes the active possible. [Era Organics](https://share.google/fWOIAsm7BkSsb8DKy) uses it as the chloride source for the hypochlorous acid in its Happy Skin spray.

Three physical attributes explain why this salt behaves the way it does. Its molecular weight is 58.44 g/mol, a small, light molecule. Its melting point is 801°C, which is why solid salt is stable at room temperature and only melts under intense heat. Its solubility in water is high — about 357 g/L at 20°C — so a large amount dissolves in a small volume of water. That high solubility is exactly what a hypochlorous acid process needs: a salt that goes fully into solution.

The chloride is the key part. When electricity passes through the salt water, that chloride is what becomes hypochlorous acid. Without salt, there is no hypochlorous acid to make.

## How salt becomes a skin-native antimicrobial

The path from salt to active is short and runs on a saltwater solution rather than pure water. A sodium chloride solution — plain water with a small amount of salt dissolved in it — is the starting point, because pure water or distilled water carries no chloride and cannot form the acid. Dissolve the salt, run a current through it, and the chloride recombines into [hypochlorous acid](/ingredients/hypochlorous-acid). This sodium chloride electrolysis is the whole basis of the method, covered in full in [electrolyzed water](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/electrolyzed-water).

What reaches the skin is the acid, not a salty solution. The salt is consumed in the reaction, and the finished spray lists only electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid at 0.02% (200 ppm). The role of sodium chloride in spray production ends once the electrolysis is complete, which is why the formula stays at two ingredients.

## Why a simple precursor keeps the formula clean

Simple inputs make a simple product. Water, salt, and electricity produce the active with no synthetic carriers or preservatives. Fewer inputs mean fewer things to irritate skin, and starting from a common, well-understood salt rather than an exotic chemical is part of that restraint.

The distinction from plain water matters here. Distilled water alone would be inert — it takes the chloride from dissolved salt to generate the acid at all. Once that job is done, the salt is spent, and the finished mist is not a saline solution but a gentle, two-ingredient product. This is why the ingredient chain is worth understanding: the properties of the salt going in shape the product coming out. A salt that dissolves fully and carries current cleanly gives a predictable reaction, and a predictable reaction gives a consistent active level in every bottle. The high solubility and simple ionic makeup of sodium chloride are exactly the traits that make it dependable in this role, where an impure or poorly soluble precursor would introduce variables the process is designed to avoid.

## Frequently asked questions about sodium chloride

### Is there salt in the hypochlorous acid spray?

Salt is the starting material, not a listed ingredient in the finished spray. It is converted during electrolysis into hypochlorous acid. The product contains electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid at 0.02% (200 ppm).

### Why is sodium chloride needed?

Its chloride is the building block of hypochlorous acid. The electrolysis process turns that chloride into the active molecule. No salt — and no chloride source that pure water or distilled water could provide — means no way to generate the acid.

### Will the spray feel salty or dry my skin?

No. The salt is used up making the active, so the spray is not a saline solution. It is a gentle, two-ingredient mist suited to daily use on sensitive skin.
