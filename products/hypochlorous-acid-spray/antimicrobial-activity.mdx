## What is antimicrobial activity?

Antimicrobial activity is the ability of a substance to reduce or slow the growth of microbes. On skin, that means lowering the load of bacteria, viruses, and fungi on the surface. ## How does hypochlorous acid reduce microbes?

Hypochlorous acid works by oxidation. Its small, uncharged shape lets it reach the cell walls and proteins microbes depend on, and it breaks them on contact. This is why it acts across bacteria, viruses, and fungi rather than one narrow target.

The effect has been measured against other antiseptics. A 2018 in vitro study compared [0.01% hypochlorous acid with povidone iodine, chlorhexidine, and isopropyl alcohol](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29985866/) on common skin microorganisms. A 2025 study found an [acid-oxidising hypochlorous acid solution reduced Staphylococcus aureus and improved the microbiome in fragile skin wounds](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40803690/).
