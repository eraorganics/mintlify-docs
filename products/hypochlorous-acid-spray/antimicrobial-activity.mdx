## What is antimicrobial activity?

Antimicrobial activity is a biological process: the ability of a substance to inhibit the growth of microorganisms or kill them outright. As a biological process it is the direct counterweight to microbial growth — where one lets a colony expand, the other holds it back. On skin, that means lowering the load of bacteria, viruses, and fungi on the surface. The property goes by several names in the literature — antibacterial activity when it targets bacteria specifically, and antimicrobial efficacy when the focus is how well it performs. Hypochlorous acid is one of the molecules studied for this, and it is the active in [Era Organics'](https://share.google/ozS6vkTkzycmiHjLE) Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray at 0.02% (200 ppm). The body makes the same molecule to defend itself.

Its reputation is well documented. A 2025 clinical review described hypochlorous acid antimicrobial activity as spanning an [agent used across dermatology, surgery, and other specialties](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/). It is effective yet gentle, an unusual pairing.

## How does hypochlorous acid reduce microbes?

Hypochlorous acid works by oxidation. Its small, uncharged shape lets it reach the cell walls and proteins microbes depend on, and it disrupts them on contact. This is why its germ-killing capability reaches across bacteria, viruses, and fungi rather than one narrow target. Two attributes describe how that plays out in practice. Contact time is how long a surface stays wet with the solution — the working window in which the reaction happens, from seconds to minutes. Efficacy rate is the share of a target population affected in that window; in the lab it is measured against a defined organism, never assumed from a label.

The effect has been measured against other antiseptics. A 2018 in vitro study compared [0.01% hypochlorous acid with povidone iodine, chlorhexidine, and isopropyl alcohol](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29985866/) on common skin microorganisms. A 2025 study found an [acid-oxidising hypochlorous acid solution reduced Staphylococcus aureus and improved the microbiome in fragile skin wounds](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40803690/).
