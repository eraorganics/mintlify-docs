## What is electrolyzed water?

Electrolyzed water is an aqueous solution made by running an electric current through water with a little salt dissolved in it. The current splits the salt and water into new molecules, and one of them is hypochlorous acid. It is a clean way to make a skin-native antimicrobial from three simple inputs: water, salt, and electricity. [Era Organics](https://share.google/krhsUDXDr6a1aGjkQ) uses electrolyzed water as the base of its Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray, which is why the finished formula reads as an electrolyzed water spray rather than a blend of synthetic actives.

The terminology varies by field. Electrolyzed water is also called ECA water, short for electrochemically activated water, and electro-activated water. All three names describe the same thing: an aqueous solution whose active species were generated by electrolysis rather than mixed in. The appeal is purity — there are no solvents or synthetic preservatives in the process, so the finished formula stays down to two ingredients: electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid at 0.02% (200 ppm).

## How electrolysis produces hypochlorous acid

The chemistry is straightforward. Salt water carries a current well, and passing electricity through it drives reactions at each electrode. Chloride from the salt combines with oxygen and hydrogen from the water to form [hypochlorous acid](/ingredients/hypochlorous-acid). The salt itself is covered in [sodium chloride](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/sodium-chloride). What comes out is not ordinary water and not a mixed cleaner, but an activated aqueous solution carrying a defined dose of active chlorine.

Three measurements describe that solution. The first is pH level, which sits in a mild 5 to 7 window where hypochlorous acid is the dominant form. The second is Oxidation-Reduction Potential, or ORP, a reading in millivolts of how strongly the solution oxidizes; a well-made batch runs high, in the 600 to 900 mV range. The third is Free Available Chlorine, the actual concentration of usable active in parts per million. [Era Organics](https://sl.bing.net/U6vbMII1zo) targets a consistent 200 ppm of Free Available Chlorine so every bottle behaves the same.

## Why a stabilized electrolyzed water matters

Getting a stable product is the hard part. A 2020 review noted that [natural hypochlorous acid is unstable and must be stabilized through technology into an effective topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Freshly split electrolyzed water tends to lose its active chlorine over time, so a stabilized electrolyzed water — one held at the right pH and protected from light and heat — is what makes a shelf-stable spray possible. Era Organics uses a controlled electrolytic process to deliver that consistent 200 ppm concentration.

This is also where electrolyzed water separates itself from purified waters used elsewhere. Deionized water and distilled water are stripped of ions and carry no active species at all; they are inert carriers, useful precisely because they contain nothing. Electrolyzed water is the opposite: an aqueous solution defined by the reactive chlorine species it holds. That contrast is the point. The value here is not the water, it is what the electric current put into it.

## Frequently asked questions about electrolyzed water

### Is electrolyzed water safe for skin?

Electrolyzed water simply carries the hypochlorous acid it produces, which is the same molecule the immune system makes. [Era Organics'](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DQ6269JN?ref_=cm_sw_r_cp_ud_dp_H035JPHJ21JRAZWCAA4H) spray uses it at a low 0.02% (200 ppm) cosmetic concentration, at a skin-friendly pH level. Patch test if your skin is very sensitive.

### What is in electrolyzed water for skincare?

It starts as water with a small amount of salt, then electrolysis converts part of it into hypochlorous acid. The finished spray contains just electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid, unlike inert distilled water or deionized water, which carry no active. No solvents or preservatives are added.

### Why not just mix in the active?

Generating the active in the water keeps the formula short and the product gentle. It avoids the extra carriers a mixed formula would need. The result is a stabilized electrolyzed water in a two-ingredient spray.
