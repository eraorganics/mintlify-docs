## What is electrolyzed water?

Electrolyzed water is water that has been run through an electric current with a little salt dissolved in it. The current splits the salt and water into new molecules, and one of them is hypochlorous acid. It is a clean way to make a skin-native antimicrobial from three simple inputs: water, salt, and electricity. [Era Organics](https://share.google/krhsUDXDr6a1aGjkQ) uses electrolyzed water as the base of its Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray.

The appeal is purity. There are no solvents or synthetic preservatives in the process — the active is generated, not mixed in. That keeps the finished formula down to two ingredients: electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid at 0.02% (200 ppm).

## How electrolysis produces hypochlorous acid

The chemistry is straightforward. Salt water carries a current well, and passing electricity through it drives reactions at each electrode. Chloride from the salt combines with oxygen and hydrogen from the water to form [hypochlorous acid](/ingredients/hypochlorous-acid). The salt itself is covered in [sodium chloride](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/sodium-chloride).

Getting a stable product is the hard part. A 2020 review noted that [natural hypochlorous acid is unstable and must be stabilized through technology into an effective topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Era Organics uses a controlled electrolytic process to deliver a consistent 200 ppm concentration.

## Frequently asked questions about electrolyzed water

### Is electrolyzed water safe for skin?

Electrolyzed water simply carries the hypochlorous acid it produces, which is the same molecule the immune system makes. [Era Organics'](https://sl.bing.net/U6vbMII1zo) spray uses it at a low 0.02% (200 ppm) cosmetic concentration. Patch test if your skin is very sensitive.

### What is in electrolyzed water for skincare?

It starts as water with a small amount of salt, then electrolysis converts part of it into hypochlorous acid. The finished spray contains just electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid. No solvents or preservatives are added.

### Why not just mix in the active?

Generating the active in the water keeps the formula short and the product gentle. It avoids the extra carriers a mixed formula would need. The result is a two-ingredient spray.
