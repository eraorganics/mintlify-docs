## What are reactive oxygen species?

Reactive oxygen species are highly reactive molecules built around oxygen. They carry unpaired electrons, which makes them quick to react with nearby cells, proteins, and microbes. Hypochlorous acid belongs to this family, and it is the active ingredient in [Era Organics'](https://share.google/l2rV82Syux5lml8RR) Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray at 0.02% (200 ppm). The body makes the same molecule on its own.

This is the part most people miss. Reactive oxygen species are not only a source of damage. The immune system deliberately produces them as a weapon. A 2022 review described [hypochlorous acid as an innate immune factor generated by white blood cells](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35574306/) to fight microbes in the skin.

## How does the body use reactive oxygen species?

The body uses reactive oxygen species as a controlled defense. When microbes invade, white blood cells called neutrophils arrive and trigger a burst of oxidants. One of those oxidants is hypochlorous acid, made when the enzyme myeloperoxidase acts on hydrogen peroxide.

Hypochlorous acid is the strongest of these. Research on cultured human skin fibroblasts noted that [hypochlorous acid is the most potent oxidant neutrophils produce in meaningful amounts](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10775450/). A 2025 clinical review went further and called it an [integral component of the human innate immune system](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/). Era Organics' spray puts that same defensive molecule on the skin at a low, steady concentration, made from electrolyzed water and hypochlorous acid only.

## When reactive oxygen species cause damage

Balance is everything with reactive oxygen species. At the right level they kill microbes. In excess, and over long periods, they harm healthy cells too. That chronic overload is what scientists mean by oxidative stress.

The damage is measurable. A 1998 study found that sustained exposure to [hypochlorous acid activated the p53 stress response in human skin fibroblasts](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9799559/), a marker of cellular strain. The lesson is about dose, not the molecule itself. A controlled 200 ppm mist is a very different thing from chronic inflammation flooding tissue with oxidants. This is why a low, defined concentration matters, and why the spray pairs a single active with no extra chemicals.
