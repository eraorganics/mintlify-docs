## What is a spray delivery system?

A spray delivery system is a dispensing mechanism — the nozzle, pump, and bottle that atomize a liquid and disperse it as a fine, even mist through a nozzle. It is also called a spray mechanism or an atomization system, and all three names point to the same job: turning a liquid into droplets that land evenly on a surface. For skincare, this dispensing mechanism decides how the product actually touches the skin. [Era Organics](https://share.google/7g0Gu4tgcq7aelXbI) chose a spray for Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray because the delivery method matters as much as the active inside it.

Three attributes describe how a spray behaves. The first is particle size distribution, typically in the range of 10 to 100 microns for a skincare mist — small enough to feel like a fine fog rather than large droplets. The second is flow rate, the volume in millilitres released per press of the trigger, which sets how much lands with each pass. The third is spray pattern angle, usually 15 to 45 degrees, the cone the mist fans out into. Together these attributes describe a fine mist spray delivery system that lays product down evenly.

The reason a spray suits this product is contact. A mist covers skin without a hand, a cotton pad, or any rubbing. For sensitive or freshly treated skin, that no-touch application is the whole point.

## The attributes that shape an even mist

Particle size distribution is the first attribute worth understanding. Droplets in the 10 to 100 micron band spread as a soft fog that settles evenly rather than pooling in one spot. That even settling is what lets a spray coat an area without heavy, uneven patches.

Flow rate is the second attribute. The millilitres released per trigger press set how much product reaches the skin in one pass, which is why a controlled pump matters — too much floods the skin, too little leaves gaps. A trigger spray delivery mechanism is designed to release a consistent amount each time.

Spray pattern angle is the third attribute. A wider cone covers more area per press; a narrower one is more targeted. An adjustable spray delivery system can shift between the two, but even a fixed pattern in the 15 to 45 degree range covers face and body cleanly.
