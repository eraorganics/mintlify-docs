## What are bacterial skin infections?

Bacterial skin infections happen when bacteria multiply in or on the skin faster than the body can control them. Staphylococcus aureus is the most common culprit. These infections need medical attention, and often antibiotics. Prevention, though, is largely about keeping the surface clean and the barrier intact.

That prevention angle is where hypochlorous acid appears in the research. It is a broad-spectrum oxidant the immune system produces itself. ## How hypochlorous acid reduces surface bacteria

Hypochlorous acid works by oxidizing bacteria on contact. It reaches the cell walls that microbes depend on and breaks them. A 2025 study found an [acid-oxidising hypochlorous acid solution reduced Staphylococcus aureus and improved the microbiome in fragile skin wounds](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40803690/).

Its reach extends to hard cases. A 2024 study showed a [hypochlorous-acid-producing device stayed active against a polymicrobial wound infection involving MRSA and biofilms](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39162542/) in mice. A 2025 systematic review placed hypochlorous acid among the [topical antiseptics assessed for chronic wound bacteria](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39355996/).
