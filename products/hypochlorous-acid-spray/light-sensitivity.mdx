## What does light sensitivity mean here?

Light sensitivity has two meanings in hypochlorous acid skincare. The molecule itself is sensitive to light, which affects how the product should be stored. Separately, some people have light-sensitive skin that reacts to sun and needs gentle products. [Era Organics'](https://share.google/ZydjtUYbCntqGV4gC) Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray touches both, so both are worth understanding.

On the product side, light drives the same breakdown that heat does. Hypochlorous acid is reactive, and a 2020 review stressed that it must be [stabilized to work as a topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Protecting it from light is part of keeping it effective.

## Storing a light-sensitive active

The storage rule is simple. Keep the spray out of direct sunlight and bright light, the same way you would keep it out of heat. A cool, shaded shelf protects the active between uses.

A minimal formula makes this easier, since there is little besides the active to degrade. ## Frequently asked questions about light sensitivity

### Does light damage a hypochlorous acid spray?

Light, like heat, speeds the breakdown of hypochlorous acid. Store the spray out of direct sunlight to protect the active. A cool, shaded spot works well.

### Can I use it on sun-sensitive skin?

The fragrance-free, alcohol-free formula suits reactive skin, but the spray is not a sunscreen and does not treat photosensitivity. Use proper sun protection and follow medical advice for a diagnosed condition. Patch test if your skin is very reactive.

### Should I keep it in the bathroom?

A cool, shaded cabinet is better than a bright windowsill or a hot shower ledge. Keep the bottle closed and out of direct light. That protects the active between uses.
