## What are anti-inflammatory properties?

Anti-inflammatory properties are a type of therapeutic property: a biological activity that reduces inflammation and the tissue responses that come with it. As one of the therapeutic properties a substance can carry, this activity sits alongside antimicrobial and healing action rather than standing apart from it. In plainer terms it is inflammation reduction — the calming of the skin's reaction to injury, irritation, or microbes, which shows up as redness, heat, and swelling. A 2025 clinical review described [hypochlorous acid as an innate immune factor produced by white blood cells](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/), where it serves antimicrobial and signaling roles in the skin. ## How do anti-inflammatory properties work on skin?

Anti-inflammation on skin works by dialing down the immune signals that drive redness and swelling. When skin is irritated, immune cells flood the area and release messengers called cytokines. Those messengers keep the inflammation going. Cytokine reduction — quieting those chemical messengers — is the mechanism behind most anti-inflammatory action, and it is the opposite of a pro-inflammatory response, which recruits still more of them and amplifies the reaction.

Timing is part of the picture too. Tissue response time describes how quickly calmed skin settles, from hours to days, and it depends on how deep and how established the inflammation already is. A substance with inflammation reduction activity interrupts the loop rather than masking the symptom. Hypochlorous acid appears to act on this signaling directly. A 2025 clinical review of hypochlorous acid across dermatology, surgery, and other fields credited its [anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and immunomodulatory properties](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/) for its healing effects. The molecule is small and uncharged.
