## What is skin regeneration?

Skin regeneration is the process by which the skin repairs and rebuilds its own cells. It runs constantly, and it speeds up after damage from a wound or a procedure. A clean, low-inflammation surface lets that process work. [Era Organics](https://share.google/JeuM8GfM85tFnhFAs) supports that environment with hypochlorous acid, the active in its Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray at 0.02% (200 ppm).

Hypochlorous acid does not regrow skin — the skin does that. What the molecule does is clear obstacles. A 2020 review noted that hypochlorous acid [increases oxygenation at the wound site to improve healing](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/), alongside broad antimicrobial action.

## How a clean surface supports repair

Bacteria and inflammation both slow regeneration. They keep the immune system busy fighting instead of rebuilding. Lowering that load frees the skin to repair.

Research ties hypochlorous acid to exactly this. A 2025 study found an [acid-oxidising hypochlorous acid solution reduced Staphylococcus aureus and restored a healthier microbiome in fragile skin wounds](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40803690/). A 2025 facial laser study reported a [hypochlorous acid mist improved healing and cosmesis after the procedure](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40832716/).
