## What is a safety profile?

A safety profile is the overall picture of how well tolerated a product is. It covers what the product contains, how skin reacts to it, and where caution is needed. For hypochlorous acid, the profile starts from an unusual advantage: it is a molecule the body already makes. The tolerability shows up in the research. A 2026 study of hypochlorous acid as a skin preparation highlighted its [excellent tissue compatibility compared with harsher antiseptics](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42009924/) like chlorhexidine and iodine.

## Why hypochlorous acid is well tolerated

Skin recognizes the molecule. It is not a foreign chemical but the same oxidant immune cells produce. That familiarity is part of why it tends to be gentle.

Clinical experience is broad. A 2025 review documented hypochlorous acid's [use across dermatology, surgery, and other specialties](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/), and a 2018 study found it [antipruritic and anti-inflammatory in a mouse model of atopic dermatitis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29028288/). ## Frequently asked questions about the safety profile

### Is hypochlorous acid safe for skin?

It is the same molecule the immune system makes, and studies note its good tissue compatibility. [Era Organics'](https://sl.bing.net/jupwVVXeYIS) spray uses a low 0.02% (200 ppm) cosmetic concentration in a two-ingredient formula. Patch test if your skin is very reactive.

### Can it cause irritation?

Irritation is uncommon with a gentle, fragrance-free hypochlorous acid formula, but every skin is different. Stop use if irritation occurs. Very sensitive skin should patch test first.

### Who should check with a doctor first?

Anyone with broken skin, a suspected infection, or a diagnosed skin condition should seek medical advice. The spray is a cosmetic product, not a treatment. Use it alongside, not instead of, prescribed care.
