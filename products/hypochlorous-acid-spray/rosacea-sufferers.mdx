## What is rosacea?

Rosacea is a chronic inflammatory skin condition marked by facial redness, visible vessels, and sometimes bumps. It flares easily and reacts to many common skincare ingredients. A 2011 review described rosacea's core features as [redness, telangiectasia, and a large inflammatory component](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22183097/). Managing it means calming triggers and avoiding harsh products.

Because inflammation is central, rosacea-prone skin does best with gentle care. ## Why gentle matters for rosacea-prone skin

Rosacea skin overreacts. A 2023 study tied rosacea to [cutaneous neurogenic inflammation](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37606610/), the kind of hair-trigger response that fragrance and alcohol can set off. The first rule of rosacea care is to stop provoking the skin.

Hypochlorous acid fits because it is both gentle and anti-inflammatory. A 2025 review documented its [anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial use in dermatology](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/).
