## What is immune response modulation?

Immune response modulation is the adjustment of immune activity — turning it up against threats or down when it overreacts. Skin relies on both directions. It must attack microbes yet avoid the runaway inflammation that damages tissue. Hypochlorous acid sits at this balance point, and it is the active in [Era Organics'](https://share.google/rpm5lYtrrY9rsyX7s) Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray at 0.02% (200 ppm).

The molecule is native to the immune system. A 2025 clinical review described hypochlorous acid as an [integral component of the innate immune system with immunomodulatory properties](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/). It is a tool the body already uses to manage its own response.

## How hypochlorous acid works both directions

Immune cells make hypochlorous acid to fight microbes, so it is part of the attack. A 2022 review called it an [innate immune factor produced by white blood cells](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35574306/). That is the up direction.

It also helps quiet an overactive response. A 2021 study reported that [topical hypochlorous acid blocked inflammatory gene expression in mouse skin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34144392/), and a 2018 study found it was [antipruritic and anti-inflammatory in a mouse model of atopic dermatitis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29028288/). That is the down direction.
