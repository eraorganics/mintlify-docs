## What are inflammatory skin conditions?

Inflammatory skin conditions are disorders where the immune system drives redness, swelling, and irritation in the skin. Eczema, rosacea, and acne all belong to this group. They differ in their triggers but share an overactive inflammatory response. Managing them well usually means calming that response without adding new irritants.

Hypochlorous acid is studied in this context because it is both an antimicrobial and an anti-inflammatory molecule. ## How hypochlorous acid acts on inflammation

Inflammation runs on gene signals inside skin cells. Quiet those signals and the redness settles. A 2021 study reported that [topical hypochlorous acid blocked inflammatory gene expression in UV-exposed mouse skin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34144392/).

The itch side has been studied too. A 2018 study found [hypochlorous acid was antipruritic and anti-inflammatory in a mouse model of atopic dermatitis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29028288/), and noted that topical HOCl had relieved itch in human patients. A 2025 clinical review confirmed its [anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial use across dermatology](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/).
