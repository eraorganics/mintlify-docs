## How does hypochlorous acid disinfect skin?

Hypochlorous acid disinfects by oxidizing the microbes it touches. It is a small, neutral molecule, so it reaches the cell walls and proteins that bacteria depend on. That oxidation breaks them apart on contact.

Its efficacy has been measured directly against other antiseptics. A 2018 in vitro study compared [0.01% hypochlorous acid against povidone iodine, chlorhexidine, and isopropyl alcohol](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29985866/) on common skin microorganisms. A 2025 clinical review across several medical fields confirmed its use as an [antimicrobial agent in dermatology and surgery](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/). ## Frequently asked questions about skin disinfection

### What is the difference between disinfecting and cleansing skin?

Cleansing removes dirt and oil from the skin. Disinfecting reduces the living microbes on it. A wash can do both, but an antiseptic is made specifically to lower microbial load before a procedure or after a wound.
