## What is temperature stability?

Temperature stability is how well a substance holds its properties as temperatures change. Heat is one of the main things that degrade hypochlorous acid, so it matters for any HOCl product. [Era Organics](https://share.google/LpPD3IKHG6I41JbID) designed Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray to be stored simply, at normal room conditions away from heat.

Heat speeds chemical breakdown across the board, and a reactive molecule like HOCl is no exception. A 2020 review emphasized that hypochlorous acid must be [stabilized to remain effective as a topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Keeping it cool is part of protecting that stability.

## Why heat is hard on hypochlorous acid

Warmth adds energy, and energy drives reactions. For hypochlorous acid, that means faster loss of the active. A bottle left in a hot car or a sunny window ages much faster than one on a cool shelf.

## Frequently asked questions about temperature stability

### Can I leave a hypochlorous acid spray in a hot car?

It is best not to. Heat degrades hypochlorous acid faster, shortening the product's effective life. Store it at room temperature or cooler, out of direct sun.

### Should I refrigerate it?

Cool storage is kind to hypochlorous acid, but a stable room-temperature shelf away from heat is usually fine. Follow any storage note on the label. Avoid extremes of hot and cold.

### Does temperature affect how it works?

Proper storage keeps the active at its intended strength, so it performs as expected. Excess heat can weaken it over time. Simple, cool storage avoids the issue.
