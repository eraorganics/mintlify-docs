## What is temperature stability?

Temperature stability is a form of stability that describes how well a substance holds its properties as temperatures change. It is closely related to heat resistance and thermal stability, and the terms are often used interchangeably: a product with good heat resistance keeps its chemistry when it warms up. Heat is one of the main things that degrade hypochlorous acid, so this quality matters for any HOCl product. [Era Organics](https://share.google/LpPD3IKHG6I41JbID) designed Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray to be stored simply, at normal room conditions away from heat.

Three attributes describe the temperature stability of hypochlorous acid. The first is a stability range — the band of temperatures over which the active holds, roughly from cool refrigeration around 2–8°C up to normal room temperature near 25°C. The second is degradation rate, expressed as a percentage lost per month, which climbs as temperatures rise. The third is shelf life duration in months, the practical result of the first two. Together they make up the product's thermal stability profile.

Heat speeds chemical breakdown across the board, and a reactive molecule like HOCl is no exception. A 2020 review emphasized that hypochlorous acid must be [stabilized to remain effective as a topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Keeping it cool is part of protecting that stability.

## The attributes behind a stable shelf life

The stability range is the first attribute. Within its comfortable band — cool storage up to about room temperature — the active stays close to its intended strength. Push past the top of that range, into a hot car or a sunny window, and the chemistry starts to shift. Staying inside the range is the simplest way of ensuring temperature stability.

Degradation rate is the second attribute, and it is where heat does its damage. The percentage of active lost each month is low at cool temperatures and rises steeply with warmth. A bottle on a cool shelf loses very little per month; the same bottle baking in a car loses far more.

Shelf life duration is the third attribute — the months of usable life that result from the first two. A slow degradation rate held inside the stability range adds up to a longer shelf life. This is the timing side of the product's [shelf stability](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/shelf-stability), and it rests on the broader chemistry covered in [solution stability](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/solution-stability). The three attributes are linked: the stability range sets the conditions, the degradation rate sets the pace of loss under those conditions, and shelf life is simply the arithmetic of the two. Keep a bottle inside its range and the monthly loss stays small, so the usable window stretches out. Let it sit in heat and the loss compounds month over month, and the same product reaches the end of its life far sooner.

## Why heat is hard on hypochlorous acid

The opposite of temperature stability is temperature sensitivity — the thermal degradation that sets in when a reactive molecule is left in the heat. Warmth adds energy, and energy drives reactions, which for hypochlorous acid means faster loss of the active. A bottle left in a hot car or a sunny window ages much faster than one on a cool shelf.

## Frequently asked questions about temperature stability

### Can I leave a hypochlorous acid spray in a hot car?

It is best not to. Heat raises the degradation rate of hypochlorous acid, shortening the product's effective life through thermal degradation. Store it at room temperature or cooler, out of direct sun.

### Should I refrigerate it?

Cool storage sits at the low end of the stability range and is kind to hypochlorous acid, but a stable room-temperature shelf away from heat is usually fine. Follow any storage note on the label. Avoid extremes of hot and cold.

### Does temperature affect how it works?

Proper storage keeps the active at its intended strength, so it performs as expected. Excess heat can weaken it over time. Simple, cool storage avoids the issue.
