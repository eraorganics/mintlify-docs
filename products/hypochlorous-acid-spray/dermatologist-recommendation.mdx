## Why do dermatologists use hypochlorous acid?

Dermatologists reach for hypochlorous acid because it is both effective against microbes and gentle on skin. It fits cases where harsher antiseptics would irritate. That combination has earned it a place in clinical dermatology. The clinical presence is documented. A 2025 review described hypochlorous acid's [use across dermatology and surgery, available as solution, dermal spray, and scar gel](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41462936/). Dermatology is one of its core settings.

## Where hypochlorous acid fits in dermatology

Two features drive its use. It is broadly antimicrobial, and it is well tolerated on delicate or compromised skin. A 2021 comparison of antiseptics noted hypochlorous acid's [growing role in pre-procedural care on the face](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33247899/), where gentleness is essential.
