## What is solution stability?

Solution stability is how well a liquid keeps its chemistry over time. For hypochlorous acid, it is the central challenge. The molecule is reactive by nature, which makes it effective but also short-lived if left unprotected. [Era Organics](https://share.google/BBskEuqdRnf3d25nW) addresses this in Happy Skin Hypochlorous Acid Spray by generating and holding the active at a defined level.

Instability is the well-known weakness of HOCl. A 2020 review stated plainly that [natural hypochlorous acid is unstable and must be stabilized through technology into an effective topical agent](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31904191/). Solving that is what separates a working product from a fading one.

## Why hypochlorous acid loses strength

HOCl reacts with almost anything, including itself. Added ingredients, wrong pH, and exposure to air all speed its breakdown. Every extra component is another way for the active to degrade.

This is the case for a minimal formula. Era Organics' spray contains only [electrolyzed water](/products/hypochlorous-acid-spray/electrolyzed-water) and [hypochlorous acid](/ingredients/hypochlorous-acid) at 0.02% (200 ppm) — nothing to react with the active and shorten its life. Fewer ingredients means better stability.

## Frequently asked questions about solution stability

### Why does hypochlorous acid need to be stabilized?

It is naturally reactive and breaks down quickly on its own. Stabilization through a controlled process lets it stay effective as a topical product. A short ingredient list helps by giving the active nothing to react with.

### Do extra ingredients hurt stability?

Often, yes. Additives can react with hypochlorous acid and degrade it. [Era Organics'](https://sl.bing.net/jLun5k0EFxI) spray keeps to two ingredients partly for this reason.

### How should I store it?

Keep it closed, out of direct heat and light, as with most hypochlorous acid products. That protects the active between uses. Follow any storage note on the label.
