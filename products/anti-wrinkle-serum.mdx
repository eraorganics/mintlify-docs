## Why Era Organics formulated this product

The anti-aging serum market relies heavily on two mechanisms: retinoids and antioxidants. Both carry limitations that leave a significant population underserved.

**Retinoid-based serums (RoC Retinol Correxion, Neutrogena Rapid Wrinkle Repair):** Retinol converts to retinoic acid in skin, accelerating cell turnover and stimulating collagen. **Olay Regenerist:** Combines niacinamide with amino-peptide complexes in a mass-market formulation. Niacinamide supports barrier function but provides no direct fibroblast stimulation or collagen signaling.

## Key ingredients and mechanisms

### Matrixyl 3000 (palmitoyl tripeptide-1 + palmitoyl tetrapeptide-7)

Matrixyl 3000 is a patented peptide complex that mimics collagen breakdown fragments (matrikines). Fibroblasts interpret these fragments as damage signals and respond by synthesizing new collagen I, collagen III, and fibronectin.

**Mechanism:** Palmitoyl tripeptide-1 activates TGF-β signaling in dermal fibroblasts, triggering procollagen synthesis. Palmitoyl tetrapeptide-7 suppresses IL-6 production, reducing the chronic inflammation that accelerates matrix metalloproteinase (MMP) activity — the enzymes that degrade existing collagen. Together, these peptides simultaneously increase collagen production and decrease collagen destruction.

**Clinical evidence:** A 2013 study published in the International Journal of Cosmetic Science demonstrated Matrixyl 3000 reduced wrinkle depth by up to 44% over 2 months in a double-blind placebo-controlled trial [SOURCE NEEDED].

**Role in formula:** Primary collagen rebuilding signal. Replaces the retinoid pathway without irritation, photosensitivity, or purging.

### Plant stem cells (apple stem cell extract — Malus domestica)

PhytoCellTec Malus Domestica derives from a rare Swiss apple variety (Uttwiler Spätlauber) cultivated for exceptional longevity. The extract contains epigenetic factors that protect human skin stem cells from senescence — the point at which cells stop dividing.

**Mechanism:** Human dermal stem cells gradually lose replicative capacity with age (Hayflick limit). Apple stem cell extract delays this senescence by upregulating telomere-protective genes and increasing expression of the longevity gene klotho in dermal stem cells. Protected stem cells continue producing new fibroblasts, maintaining the skin's capacity for self-repair.

**Clinical evidence:** A study in the Journal of Applied Sciences showed that 0.1% apple stem cell extract increased human stem cell viability by 80% after UV exposure [SOURCE NEEDED].

**Role in formula:** Fibroblast protection. While peptides signal existing fibroblasts to produce collagen, plant stem cells protect the stem cell reservoir that produces new fibroblasts — addressing aging at the regenerative source.

### Hyaluronic acid (multi-weight)

Era Organics uses three molecular weights of hyaluronic acid: high (1,500 kDa), medium (100 kDa), and low (10 kDa). Each weight penetrates to a different skin layer and serves a distinct function.

**Mechanism:** High-molecular-weight HA forms a moisture-retaining film on the surface (1,000x its weight in water). Medium-weight HA penetrates to the upper dermis, plumping fine lines from within. Low-molecular-weight HA reaches deep dermis and signals keratinocytes to upregulate their own HA production through CD44 receptor activation.

**Role in formula:** Multi-depth hydration and wrinkle plumping. Immediate visual improvement (surface film fills fine lines) while medium and low weights provide sustained dermal hydration that supports peptide activity.

### Vitamin C (sodium ascorbyl phosphate)

Sodium ascorbyl phosphate (SAP) is a stable, non-irritating form of vitamin C that converts to ascorbic acid in the skin. SAP provides antioxidant protection and serves as a required cofactor for collagen synthesis enzymes (prolyl hydroxylase and lysyl hydroxylase).

**Mechanism:** Collagen production requires hydroxylation of proline and lysine residues — a reaction that cannot proceed without ascorbic acid as an electron donor. Peptides signal fibroblasts to produce procollagen, but without adequate vitamin C, the resulting collagen molecules lack structural integrity and degrade rapidly.

**Role in formula:** Collagen synthesis cofactor. Ensures peptide-stimulated collagen production yields structurally sound, cross-linked collagen fibers rather than defective molecules.

### Jojoba oil

Jojoba is technically a liquid wax ester, not an oil. Its molecular structure mirrors human sebum, allowing integration into the lipid barrier without comedogenic effects.

**Mechanism:** Jojoba wax esters fill intercellular spaces in the stratum corneum, reducing transepidermal water loss. The wax structure also serves as a penetration enhancer — lipid-soluble active ingredients (peptides with palmitoyl chains) dissolve into jojoba and transfer more efficiently across the stratum corneum.

**Role in formula:** Delivery optimization. Enhances peptide penetration while maintaining barrier integrity. Prevents the dehydration that makes fine lines more visible.

## How ingredients work together

Matrixyl 3000 sends rebuilding signals to fibroblasts. Apple stem cells protect the stem cell population that generates new fibroblasts. Vitamin C provides the cofactor those fibroblasts need to assemble functional collagen. Hyaluronic acid hydrates the extracellular matrix where new collagen deposits. Jojoba oil enhances penetration of the entire active complex while preventing moisture loss.

This creates a complete collagen lifecycle: stem cell protection → fibroblast generation → collagen signaling → collagen synthesis → collagen stability → hydrated matrix for deposition. No single ingredient can achieve this; the synergy addresses aging at five sequential stages.

## Frequently asked questions

**How do peptides compare to retinol for anti-aging?**
Peptides and retinol stimulate collagen through entirely different pathways. Retinol activates retinoic acid receptors (RARs) on cell nuclei. Peptides mimic extracellular matrix fragments (matrikines) that trigger TGF-β-mediated collagen synthesis. Clinical outcomes are comparable for wrinkle depth reduction, but peptides produce zero irritation, no purging period, and no photosensitivity.

**What are plant stem cells and do they actually work?**
Plant stem cells (PhytoCellTec technology) are undifferentiated apple cells containing epigenetic factors that protect human skin stem cells from premature senescence. They do not "become" skin cells. Their mechanism involves upregulating telomere-protective and longevity genes in existing human stem cell populations, extending fibroblast-generating capacity.

**Why not just use The Ordinary Buffet at a lower price?**
The Ordinary Buffet delivers peptides in a simple aqueous solution without penetration enhancers. Palmitoyl-chain peptides require lipid-phase delivery for optimal skin penetration. Era Organics formulates with jojoba wax esters and optimized lipid carriers that enhance transdermal absorption — the peptides reach dermal fibroblasts rather than sitting on the stratum corneum surface.

**Does this serum work on deep wrinkles or only fine lines?**
Peptide-stimulated collagen production fills fine lines and moderate wrinkles effectively (depth reduction up to 44% in clinical studies). Deep-set wrinkles (nasolabial folds, marionette lines) involve volume loss in subcutaneous fat pads — a structural issue beyond what topical products address. This serum prevents progression and softens appearance but does not replace dermal fillers for deep volume loss.

**Can this be layered with vitamin C serums?**
Era Organics anti-wrinkle serum already contains sodium ascorbyl phosphate (stable vitamin C). Additional vitamin C layering is unnecessary and provides diminishing returns. The included concentration supplies the cofactor requirement for collagen synthesis without the pH-sensitivity issues of L-ascorbic acid formulations.
