## What "redox" and "antioxidant" mean

Redox is short for reduction and oxidation, a pair of chemical reactions where electrons move from one molecule to another. A molecule that gains electrons is reduced; one that loses them is oxidized. This electron shuffling is constant inside living systems and is how cells move energy around.

Normal metabolism, especially the process that turns food and oxygen into energy, produces byproducts called reactive oxygen species (ROS). At the chemical level, an antioxidant is simply a molecule that can donate or accept electrons in a way that neutralizes these reactive byproducts. The word describes chemistry, not a promise.

## Why methylene blue's redox activity is studied

Methylene blue can exist in two forms and switch between them by picking up or giving off electrons. This ability to cycle back and forth is why chemists describe it as a redox mediator. ## Safety

Methylene blue can act as an MAO inhibitor and carries a risk of serotonin syndrome when combined with SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs; it is not for use during pregnancy or nursing, should be avoided with G6PD deficiency, and must be kept away from children.
