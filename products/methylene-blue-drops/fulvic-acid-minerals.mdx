## What Ionic Fulvic-Acid Minerals Are

Fulvic acid is a naturally occurring organic substance. It forms as plant matter breaks down in soil over long periods, becoming part of the dark, rich material known as humus. Chemically, it is a small, water-soluble molecule that readily carries trace minerals along with it.

When minerals are described as "ionic," it means they are present as free, individual charged particles rather than locked inside larger clumps. Fulvic acid naturally holds minerals in this ionic form. That is the sense in which the phrase "ionic fulvic-acid minerals" is used here: fulvic acid acting as a carrier for trace minerals.

## Why Fulvic Acid Is of Interest

One reason researchers find fulvic acid interesting is that it is redox-active. Redox chemistry refers to the exchange of electrons between molecules, a fundamental process in living systems. Fulvic acid can take part in these electron exchanges, which is why it has been studied and explored in laboratory research for how it behaves alongside other redox-active compounds. Any such research describes the substance itself, not this product, and does not establish any benefit.

## Safety

Methylene blue carries a risk of serotonin syndrome when combined with serotonergic medications (SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs); it is not for use during pregnancy or nursing, should be avoided with G6PD deficiency, and must be kept away from children.
