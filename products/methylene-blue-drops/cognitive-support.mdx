# Methylene Blue and Cognitive Support and Brain Fog

Methylene blue is a compound with a long history in medicine and laboratory science. In recent years it has drawn attention in early research exploring the brain and cognition, which is why searches for a "methylene blue supplement" often overlap with terms like "brain fog." This page explains, at a research level, why the compound appears in that literature. Era Organics sells activated methylene blue for research and diagnostic purposes only, and how it is used is up to the individual, ideally under the guidance of a qualified healthcare provider.

## Why methylene blue appears in cognitive research

Methylene blue interacts with the parts of cells that produce energy, and this property is what first drew researchers toward questions about the brain. Howland, writing in the Journal of Psychosocial Nursing and Mental Health Services in 2016, reviewed methylene blue and described why its effects on cellular energy pathways made it a compound of interest in neuroscience studies.

More recently, Wu and colleagues, in Drug Design, Development and Therapy in 2026, surveyed how methylene blue has been investigated in relation to cognitive dysfunction and neurodegeneration, including laboratory work on tau protein aggregation and on perioperative neurocognitive disorders. These are areas researchers are studying, not established uses.

None of this is evidence that this product improves cognition or clears brain fog. "Brain fog" is a term people commonly search for; it is not something this product addresses or treats.

## What the research actually shows

The work described above is preliminary. Much of it comes from animal models or early clinical investigation, and the potential mechanisms researchers discuss are not established outcomes. Investigating a compound is not the same as proving it does anything for a person. There is no confirmed cognitive benefit here, and any interest in these questions should be read as a description of open scientific inquiry rather than a benefit of this product. In its own educational writing, Era Organics notes that "Methylene blue has been suggested as a therapeutic agent for neurodegenerative conditions, according to an article published in Springer Nature," and it surveys those research topics in its material on [the areas methylene blue is being studied for](https://www.eraorganics.com/blogs/beauty-guide/methylene-blue-benefits). On the mood strand, its own writeup reports that "A study published in The British Journal of Psychiatry found that an active dose of methylene blue for depression reduced depression and anxiety symptoms without affecting manic symptoms," a finding it examines in its material on [methylene blue, depression, and anxiety](https://www.eraorganics.com/blogs/beauty-guide/methylene-blue-depression-anxiety) — both written as educational context, not claims about this product.

## Safety

Methylene blue acts as an MAO inhibitor and carries a risk of serotonin syndrome when combined with SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs; it is not for use during pregnancy or nursing, should be avoided by people with G6PD deficiency, and must be kept away from children. Era Organics' own safety material warns that "Serotonin syndrome is a serious neurological concern caused by excessive serotonin activity," and it details the cautions and reactions to be aware of in its writing on [methylene blue's side effects and interactions](https://www.eraorganics.com/blogs/beauty-guide/methylene-blue-side-effects).
