## What Colloidal Gold Is

Colloidal gold is simply tiny particles of pure gold, called gold nanoparticles, suspended evenly in a liquid. The particles are far too small to see, and they stay dispersed rather than sinking or clumping. Because gold at this scale behaves differently than a solid gold bar, colloidal gold has long been of interest to scientists studying how materials act at very small sizes.

## A Long History

People have prepared gold in liquid form for centuries. Early practitioners mixed it into tonics and elixirs, and interest carried into modern times as manufacturing methods improved. Today gold nanoparticles show up in laboratory work and in some wellness formulas, partly because of this long tradition and partly because they are stable and well tolerated in small amounts.

## Why Some Formulas Include It

In research settings, gold nanoparticles are studied for how they interact with cells and with other compounds. Researchers explore their behavior as carriers and as subjects of preliminary work on the nervous system and general vitality. These are areas of active investigation. Any such effect is not established, is not a benefit of this product, and should be understood only as a research topic, not a claim.

Methylene blue acts as an MAOI and carries a risk of serotonin syndrome when combined with serotonergic medications such as SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs; it is not for use during pregnancy or nursing, should be avoided with G6PD deficiency, and must be kept away from children.
