## What are natural and organic ingredients in a face scrub?

Natural and organic ingredients are the naturally derived, plant-based materials a formula is built on instead of synthetic ones. In the language of cosmetic design, a natural and organic ingredient list describes a formulation type — a choice about where the components come from rather than a single active. The same idea shows up on labels a few ways — as natural ingredients, as organic ingredients, or as a clean beauty formula — but each points to the same thing: botanical extracts, plant oils, and other naturally sourced materials doing the work. Era Organics built its Revive+ scrub around this approach, pairing a natural exfoliating grit with soothing plant ingredients.

A natural formula is made of a few recognizable component parts. There are plant extracts — botanical materials pressed or infused from plants. There are natural oils that condition the skin. And there are botanical actives, the plant-derived ingredients that carry the functional work. In the Revive+ scrub those parts include the grit from [micronized walnut shell](/products/face-exfoliation-scrub/walnut-shell-powder), the gel from [aloe vera gel](/ingredients/aloe-vera), papaya enzyme, manuka honey, and vitamin E — a lineup drawn from plants and other natural sources rather than a lab-built base.

## What "natural" and "organic" actually describe

The two words are related but not identical. "Natural" describes where an ingredient comes from — a material sourced from a plant, mineral, or other natural origin rather than synthesized from petrochemicals. "Organic" adds a farming standard on top: an organic ingredient is grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers under certified agricultural practices. So a formula can contain naturally derived ingredients, some of which are also organically grown. What matters on the label are three attributes: the ingredient sourcing (natural versus synthetic origin), the naturally derived share (how much of the formula comes from natural sources), and the plant origin of the key materials.

It is worth being precise here. Describing a scrub as made with natural and organic ingredients is a statement about the ingredients, not a certification of the finished product. Era Organics' Revive+ scrub is formulated with natural and organic ingredients; it is not being claimed as a USDA Organic or COSMOS certified product. Those are separate, audited certifications with their own rules, and the ingredient-level natural and organic sourcing described here does not by itself confer them.

## Why a natural formula suits an exfoliant

An exfoliant already asks something of the skin — it lifts away the top layer of dead cells — so what surrounds that action matters. A naturally derived formula pairs the exfoliating step with plant ingredients chosen to calm and condition rather than strip. Aloe soothes and hydrates, manuka honey acts as a humectant that draws water to the surface, and [tocopherol antioxidant](/ingredients/vitamin-e) supports the freshly exposed skin. Building the scrub around botanical actives keeps the whole product gentle, which is consistent with its wider [dual action formula](/products/face-exfoliation-scrub/dual-action-formula) and its microbead-free, sulfate-free design.

The natural approach also connects to a broader set of values many people look for — that the product is cruelty-free and free of plastic microbeads and harsh sulfates. Those are related commitments rather than the same thing, but together they describe a formula designed with both the skin and the environment in mind. That plant-based sourcing is part of what defines the [Era Organics Face Exfoliation Scrub](/products/face-exfoliation-scrub).

## Natural versus synthetic formulas

The contrast that gives the attribute meaning is the synthetic formula — a product built largely on artificial ingredients, from petroleum-derived emollients to synthetic abrasives and lab-made surfactants. There is nothing inherently unsafe about many synthetic ingredients, but a naturally derived formula is a deliberate choice to source from plants and other natural materials instead. Where an artificial-ingredient scrub might rely on plastic beads and strong detergents, a natural face scrub reaches for walnut grit, a fruit enzyme, and botanical soothers. The difference is one of sourcing philosophy, and for shoppers who prioritize purity and plant origin, it is the deciding factor.

## Frequently asked questions about natural and organic ingredients

### What is the difference between "natural" and "organic" ingredients?

"Natural" describes origin — an ingredient sourced from a plant, mineral, or other natural source rather than synthesized. "Organic" adds a farming standard, meaning the material was grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers under certified practices. A formula can be built on naturally derived ingredients, some of which are also organically grown.

### Is the Revive+ scrub USDA Organic or COSMOS certified?

The scrub is formulated with natural and organic ingredients, but that is an ingredient-level description, not a product certification. It is not being claimed as USDA Organic or COSMOS certified — those are separate audited standards with their own requirements.

### Does natural mean the scrub is allergen-free?

No. Natural ingredients can still cause reactions — this scrub contains walnut, a tree nut, and manuka honey, so it is neither tree-nut-free nor vegan. Anyone with a nut allergy should avoid it, and everyone should patch test a new product on the inner forearm before use.

### Are natural ingredients better than synthetic ones?

Not automatically — many synthetic ingredients are perfectly safe. A natural formula is a sourcing choice: building the product from plant-derived and naturally sourced materials rather than artificial ones. Whether that matters is a question of the values and preferences you bring to a product.
