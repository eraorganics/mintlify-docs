## What does fragrance-free mean?

Fragrance-free is a type of formulation attribute — a description of how a product is made rather than of a single ingredient in it. A fragrance-free formula is built without any added fragrance, meaning no perfume, no aroma compounds, and no masking scent are included to change how the product smells. The terms describe the same thing several ways: you will see a product called fragrance-free, called unscented, or described as perfume-free, and all three point to a formula made with no added fragrance. Era Organics builds its [Era Organics Vaginal Moisturizer](/products/vaginal-moisturizer) this way — a cosmetic balm for external vulvar skin that carries no fragrance oils and no masking scent.

The distinction matters because "fragrance" on a cosmetic label is not one ingredient. It is a catch-all term that can stand for dozens of individual aroma chemicals, and it is one of the most common reasons a product feels irritating on sensitive skin, a driver of [vulvar irritation and discomfort](/products/vaginal-moisturizer/vulvar-irritation). Choosing a fragrance free formula removes that whole category from the ingredient list. For delicate intimate skin, that is a deliberate design choice, not an afterthought.

## Fragrance content and irritant load

The core attribute of an unscented product is its fragrance content: there is none added. That directly lowers what formulators call the irritant load — the total burden of ingredients on the list that a reactive skin type might respond to. Added fragrance is one of the more frequent culprits behind stinging, redness, or itch on thin, sensitive skin, so leaving it out is one of the simplest ways to make a product gentler.

Vulvar skin is thinner and more permeable than skin elsewhere on the body, which is part of why an unscented intimate moisturizer is a sensible default rather than a niche preference. A formula with no added fragrance gives the skin fewer things to react to. The two component parts of this attribute are easy to state plainly: no fragrance oils, and no masking scent to cover up the natural smell of the base ingredients. When both are absent, what you smell is only the mild, characteristic scent of the plant oils and butters themselves.

## Fragrance-free versus scented and fragranced

The clearest way to understand this attribute is by its opposite. A scented or fragranced product has aroma compounds added on purpose, either to create a pleasant smell or to mask the base. Those additions are exactly what a fragrance-free formula leaves out. Where a fragranced cream asks delicate skin to tolerate extra ingredients for the sake of scent, an unscented balm asks nothing of the kind. This is the trade the attribute makes: it gives up a designed smell in exchange for a shorter, gentler ingredient list.

It is worth being precise about wording, because "unscented" is sometimes used loosely in the industry to mean a product that has masking agents added to neutralize an odor. Era Organics uses fragrance-free in its literal sense here — no added fragrance and no masking scent — so the two terms line up. The suitability for sensitive skin comes from that literal absence, not from a cover-up.

The goal of the Era balm is to soothe and moisturize external vulvar skin that feels dry, chafed, or uncomfortable — and adding scent works against that goal by introducing a common irritant. ## Frequently asked questions about fragrance-free

### Is fragrance-free the same as unscented?

In how Era Organics uses the terms, yes. Fragrance-free means no fragrance was added, and here unscented means the same — there is also no masking scent added to cover the base. Some brands use "unscented" to mean a product with masking agents, so the safest label to look for is one that states both no added fragrance and no masking scent, which is what a fragrance free formula should mean.

### Why does fragrance matter for intimate skin?

Added fragrance is one of the more common irritants on sensitive skin, and vulvar skin is thinner and more permeable than skin elsewhere. Leaving fragrance out lowers the irritant load, which is why an unscented intimate moisturizer is a reasonable default for delicate skin. It is a gentleness choice, not a medical claim.

### Will a fragrance-free balm have no smell at all?

Not necessarily. A product with no added fragrance still smells faintly of its own ingredients — the plant oils and butters in the base have a mild natural scent. What is absent is any perfume or masking scent layered on top. So a fragrance-free balm may have a soft, natural aroma without carrying any added fragrance.

### Is a fragrance-free product guaranteed not to irritate?

No formula can promise that for every person. Removing fragrance takes away one of the most common irritants, which helps, but other ingredients can still be a factor for a given individual. Patch test any new product on a small area of skin first, and stop if irritation appears.
