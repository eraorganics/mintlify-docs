## What is ragweed sensitivity?

Ragweed sensitivity is a reaction to plants in the Asteraceae family — the botanical family that includes ragweed, and also daisies, chamomile, marigold, and sunflower. It is a type of botanical allergy, meaning the immune system responds to proteins or other compounds from a plant rather than to a synthetic chemical. The terms Asteraceae sensitivity, Compositae allergy, and ragweed allergy all describe the same underlying picture: a person whose immune system is primed to react to members of this large plant family. Because the family is so broad, someone who reacts to ragweed pollen may find that other Asteraceae plants can be triggers too.

Three attributes describe this sensitivity. The first is the allergen family: Asteraceae (also called Compositae) is one of the largest plant families, and its members share related compounds. The second is cross-reactivity — the immune tendency to react to several related plants at once because their allergens resemble one another; this is why an IgE response raised against one Asteraceae plant can extend to others. The third is prevalence: sensitivity to this family is one of the recognized botanical allergies, though how strongly any one person reacts varies widely. The clear contrast is with non-allergic skin, which tolerates these botanicals without an immune reaction. This page exists so that anyone who does have this sensitivity can make an informed choice.

## Awareness and patch testing before use

If you know you have a ragweed allergy or an Asteraceae sensitivity, the sensible step before regular use is a patch test: apply a small amount to a small area of skin, such as the inner forearm, wait to see whether any redness, itching, or irritation develops, and only continue if the skin stays comfortable. This is standard, cautious practice for any new botanical product, and it is doubly worth doing when a known sensitivity is in play. Awareness first, patch test second, regular use only if the skin is happy — that is the order.

Patch testing is also a normal part of introducing any product to [delicate vulvar skin](/products/vaginal-moisturizer/vulvar-skin), where the surface is thin and reactive. ## Ragweed sensitivity versus non-allergic skin

The contrast that frames this whole topic is between ragweed-sensitive skin and non-allergic skin. Non-allergic skin has no primed IgE response to Asteraceae plants, so sunflower oil behaves like any other light plant oil on it. Sensitized skin is different: the immune system already recognizes the family, and cross-reactive allergens mean a reaction to one member can generalize to others. The connotations that belong to this subject are caution and awareness — not alarm. Most people will never react. The honest disclosure exists precisely so that the minority who might can choose with full information rather than discover a sunflower oil allergy the hard way.
