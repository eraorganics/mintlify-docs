## What is a personal lubricant?

A personal lubricant is a type of intimate product applied for on-demand slipperiness, most often during sexual activity, to reduce friction between skin and surfaces. In everyday language people call it a personal lubricant, a lube, an intimate lubricant, or a sexual lubricant, and all of those names point to the same thing: a slippery product used in the moment rather than a treatment for the skin itself. It sits in the broad family of intimate products alongside washes, wipes, and moisturizers, but its job is narrow and specific. A lubricant makes things glide; it is not designed to change how hydrated the skin is over the hours and days that follow.

Understanding the personal lubricant category matters because the phrase lubricant vs moisturizer comes up constantly, and the two are genuinely different tools. This page explains what a lubricant is, factually and neutrally, and how it differs from a leave-on daily moisturizer such as an [external vaginal moisturizer](/products/vaginal-moisturizer). Neither is better than the other in the abstract — they solve different problems, and many people use both at different times.

## What a personal lubricant is made of

Most lubricants are built around one of a few base types. A water based lubricant uses water as its main carrier, which makes it easy to rinse off and generally compatible with condoms and toys. A silicone-based lubricant uses silicone fluids that stay slippery longer and resist washing away. Oil-based options also exist, though they are less common for intimate use because oils can degrade latex. Around that base, a formula usually carries a humectant to hold a little water, and a preservative to keep the product stable once it has been opened. Those component parts — a water or silicone base, a humectant, and a preservative — are what give a lubricant its texture and shelf life.

The defining attribute of a lubricant is duration. It is meant to work now, for the length of an activity, and its intended use is that short window. It is applied in the moment, not massaged in as part of a daily routine. That on-demand, temporary character is exactly what people want from a lubricant, and it is also what separates it from a moisturizer.

## Lubricant vs moisturizer: the key contrast

The clearest way to understand a lubricant is to set it against its opposite in this category. A leave-on moisturizer, or daily moisturizer, is designed to add and hold moisture in the skin over time. You apply it and leave it, and the goal is comfort and softness that lasts through the day. A lubricant does the reverse in terms of timing: it delivers immediate slipperiness that fades once the activity ends. So a lube is temporary and situational, while a daily moisturizer is routine and cumulative.

## Choosing between them

A simple way to decide: if the problem is friction during a specific activity, a personal lubricant is the right tool. If the problem is dry, tight, or uncomfortable external skin day to day, a leave-on moisturizer is the tool built for that. They are not competitors so much as different instruments. ## Frequently asked questions about personal lubricant

### What is the difference between a lubricant and a moisturizer?

A lubricant provides on-demand slipperiness for the length of an activity, then fades. A moisturizer is a leave-on product that adds and holds moisture in the skin over time as part of a daily routine. One is temporary and situational; the other is routine and cumulative. Many people use both, for different reasons.

### Is a water based lubricant the same as an intimate moisturizer?

No. A water based lubricant is carried in water so it rinses off easily and works in the moment, but it is not formulated to keep the skin hydrated for hours. An intimate or vulva moisturizer is a leave-on product meant for lasting comfort of external skin. The base may look similar, but the intended use is different.

### Can I use a lubricant for daily dryness?

A lubricant can ease friction in the moment, but it is not designed to relieve ongoing dryness, because its effect is temporary by design. For day-to-day dryness of the external skin, a leave-on moisturizer is the tool built for that job. If dryness is new, persistent, or bothersome, it is worth talking with your doctor to understand the cause.
