## What is the COSMOS Standard?

The COSMOS Standard is a type of certification — an international standard for organic and natural cosmetics that defines what ingredients and processes qualify a finished product as organic or natural. It was created by a group of European certification bodies to give the cosmetics industry one shared, cross-border benchmark rather than a patchwork of national labels. When a product is described as COSMOS compliant, COSMOS certified, or COSMOS organic, it is being measured against that natural cosmetic standard. Era Organics states this claim for its [Era Organics Vaginal Moisturizer](/products/vaginal-moisturizer), a cosmetic balm for external vulvar skin.

Like any certification, this is a statement about how a product is formulated and sourced, not about what it does on the skin. Calling something a COSMOS compliant cosmetic says its ingredients and processing meet the standard's criteria — it does not make a health or treatment claim. The value is trust in a natural formulation, verified against published rules.

## The certifying body and the criteria scope

The first attribute of the COSMOS Standard is its certifying body — the group of certifiers behind the standard who define and uphold the criteria. That gives the certification three component parts worth naming: the criteria that set the rules, the certifier that maintains and verifies them, and the seal a qualifying product may carry. As with any credible certification, the seal is only meaningful because of the criteria and the certifier standing behind it.

The second attribute is the criteria scope — what the standard actually governs. The COSMOS Standard sets rules across the whole formulation: which ingredients are permitted, how they must be sourced, which processing methods are allowed, and how much of the content must qualify as organic or natural for a given claim. This scope is broad by design, because it aims to define "natural cosmetic" for an entire industry rather than for one product category. The criteria are what separate a genuine COSMOS certified formulation from a product that simply uses "natural" as a loose marketing word.

## A product-level certification

The third attribute is the most important one to state precisely: the COSMOS claim applies at the finished-product level. This certification describes the product as a whole — how the finished formula was built and sourced against the standard's criteria — rather than being a property of any single ingredient inside it. That is the correct way to read the claim on the Era balm: it is a product-level statement that the finished moisturizer is COSMOS compliant.

Keeping the basis at the product level matters for accuracy. It would be an overstatement to read a COSMOS claim as a promise about a specific ingredient's performance or as a medical benefit; it is neither. It is a benchmark that the finished cosmetic meets a natural cosmetic standard. Era pairs this with the [USDA organic](/products/vaginal-moisturizer/usda-organic) certification, which verifies organic content under a separate federal program — two complementary assurances about how the product is sourced and made, alongside its [cruelty-free skincare commitment](/products/vaginal-moisturizer/cruelty-free).

## Certified versus an uncertified cosmetic

The meaning of the standard is clearest against its opposite: the uncertified cosmetic. An uncertified product may describe itself as natural or organic without meeting any defined benchmark or passing any verification. A COSMOS certified cosmetic is the contrast — it is measured against published criteria maintained by a certifier and evidenced by a seal. Where an uncertified product asks you to trust a marketing word, a COSMOS compliant cosmetic points to a standard with rules behind it.

This says nothing about what the product treats, and it should not be read that way. The Era balm is a cosmetic intended to soothe and moisturize external skin; its COSMOS Standard compliance benefits from the natural ingredients the standard favors, but it remains a sourcing-and-formulation assurance, not a health claim.

## Frequently asked questions about the COSMOS Standard

### What does COSMOS certification cover?

It covers the whole formulation against a natural cosmetic standard: which ingredients are permitted, how they are sourced, what processing is allowed, and how much content must qualify as organic or natural. The criteria, the certifier, and the seal together define what a COSMOS compliant cosmetic means. It is a formulation and sourcing benchmark, not a treatment claim.

### Is COSMOS the same as USDA Organic?

No. They are separate certifications from different bodies. USDA Organic is a United States federal organic standard, while the COSMOS Standard is an international benchmark for organic and natural cosmetics created by European certifiers. A product can meet both, as the Era balm does, but each verifies a different set of criteria.

### Does COSMOS apply to a single ingredient or the whole product?

The COSMOS claim applies at the finished-product level. It describes how the whole formula was built and sourced against the standard's criteria, not the behavior of one ingredient. That product-level basis is the accurate way to read a COSMOS compliant statement on a label.

### Does COSMOS certification mean the product is more effective?

No. The standard speaks to natural and organic formulation and sourcing, not to effectiveness. A COSMOS certified cosmetic meets the benchmark's criteria; it does not promise a health outcome. It remains a cosmetic — patch test any new product on delicate skin first.
