## What are zinc oxide ointments?

Zinc oxide ointments are a class of topical ointments, thick white barrier pastes best known as classic diaper-rash creams. They also go by names like zinc cream, zinc-based ointments, and zinc paste, all describing the same white protectant. Zinc oxide forms a physical layer that shields irritated skin from moisture and friction while the skin recovers. A study using [electrometric assessment of a zinc oxide paste in diaper dermatitis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19467034/) measured its effect on the barrier and water-holding of diapered skin.

A few attributes describe these pastes. Their zinc oxide concentration commonly ranges from about 5 percent in lighter formulas up to 40 percent in heavy overnight barrier creams. Their viscosity is semi-solid, thick and opaque so it clings to the skin. And their pH sits mildly acidic to slightly alkaline, roughly 6.0 to 8.0. Those features make them well suited to barrier duty in the diaper area, though the same opaque, sticky texture is made for sealing rather than all-over daily moisturizing. That is where their role differs from a soothing everyday balm.

## Zinc oxide ointments for eczema and beyond

People sometimes reach for zinc oxide ointments for eczema-adjacent irritation as well as diaper rash, because the underlying job, protecting damaged skin from irritants, is the same whichever product does the sealing. A [systematic review of diaper dermatitis care](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41014074/) analyzed studies in nearly three thousand children and reinforced barrier protection and moisture control as the core of prevention and treatment. A zinc paste does that by laying down an occlusive white shield.

Zinc oxide is a barrier protectant, not a medicine, which sets it apart from a hydrocortisone cream that works by calming inflammation with a steroid, and from a thin moisturizing lotion that soaks in rather than sealing. Each has a niche: the steroid cream for a short inflamed flare, the lotion for light everyday hydration, and the zinc paste for a heavy protective seal on broken skin.
