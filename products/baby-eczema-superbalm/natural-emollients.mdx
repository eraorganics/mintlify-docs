## What are natural emollients?

Natural emollients are plant-derived oils and butters that soften skin and slow water loss by filling the gaps between surface cells. They are the workhorses of gentle moisturizing, and they anchor most plant-based eczema balms. Emollient therapy is a first-line habit in eczema care; a [randomized clinical trial of emollients to prevent pediatric eczema](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40699587/) tested regular emollient use in infants for its effect on developing atopic dermatitis.

Because they simply replace what dry skin has lost, emollients are safe for daily, long-term use, which is exactly what chronic eczema needs. Era Organics' Baby Eczema Superbalm is built on a base of them.

## How emollients calm the dry-skin cycle

Eczema-prone skin is caught in a loop: a leaky barrier lets water out, the skin dries and itches, scratching damages it further. Emollients break that loop by resealing the surface and easing the dryness that drives the itch. Guidance on [infant skin care](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31188166/) underscores gentle, regular moisturizing as core to keeping developing skin healthy.
