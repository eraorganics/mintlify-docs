## What is dermatological safety?

Dermatological safety is the practice of making sure a skincare product is gentle and unlikely to irritate the skin, judged through testing and careful ingredient choices. For products used on babies and eczema-prone skin, it is the whole point. Safety testing often uses patch tests; a retrospective analysis of [skin patch testing in cosmetics](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24117720/) describes how irritation-index and human patch testing are used to gauge how gentle a product is.

Era Organics approaches this from two sides: what goes in, and what stays out. Its Baby Eczema Superbalm is clinically tested and pediatrician approved, and it is built to leave out common irritants.

## Safety by what it leaves out

A big part of gentle formulation is subtraction. Superbalm is free of parabens, sulfates, phthalates, synthetic dyes, mineral oil, silicones, and drying alcohol, and it is fragrance-free and steroid-free. Removing those cuts the odds of a reaction on a barrier that is already fragile, which matters most in infancy. Guidance on [infant skin care](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31188166/) stresses gentle practices and products that support, rather than stress, developing newborn skin.

That subtraction is the same logic behind the balm's [fragrance-free](/products/baby-eczema-superbalm/fragrance-free-products) formula, extended across the whole ingredient list.

## Safe use still means patch testing

No product is right for every child, and natural ingredients can still be allergens. Superbalm contains coconut, which the FDA classifies as a tree nut, and chamomile, a member of the ragweed family, so families with those sensitivities should take care. The responsible step with any new product on a baby is a patch test first, and a check with your pediatrician or allergist if there is a known allergy.

## Frequently asked questions about dermatological safety

### Is Superbalm safe for newborns?

Superbalm is clinically tested, pediatrician approved, and formulated for delicate skin without common irritants. As with any product on a newborn, patch test first and involve your pediatrician, especially in the first weeks of life.

### How do I patch test a new balm?

Apply a small amount to a patch of skin, such as the inner forearm, and watch for redness or reaction over a day before using it more widely. Stop if the skin reacts.

### Is Superbalm hypoallergenic?

Superbalm removes many common irritants, but it is not allergen-free: it contains coconut (an FDA-classified tree nut) and chamomile (a ragweed-family botanical). Families with those allergies should patch test and consult an allergist.
