## What does non-comedogenic mean?

Non-comedogenic means a product is formulated so it is unlikely to clog pores and trigger comedones, the blackheads and whiteheads behind acne. The idea grew out of a need for cover-up products that acne-prone patients could use safely; an early study on [non-comedogenic cosmetics](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/138532/) set out to test ingredients for exactly that purpose.

It is a useful label for acne-prone facial skin. But it is worth being precise about it, because not every gentle or natural product is non-comedogenic, and Era Organics would rather be accurate than overclaim.

## Where a rich balm fits

Baby Eczema Superbalm is a rich, occlusive plant-oil balm built to seal and repair a dry, eczema-prone barrier, not a lightweight facial lotion for acne-prone skin. Some of its emollients are traditionally rated as comedogenic: a 2022 study detected [early microcomedones induced by cocoa butter](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34632684/), one of the balm's ingredients, and coconut oil is likewise considered pore-clogging for some people. For that reason we do not label Superbalm non-comedogenic.

What the balm does avoid are the synthetic pore-clogging additives some products use: it is free of mineral oil and silicones. Its emollients are [plant oils](/products/baby-eczema-superbalm/plant-oils) chosen for barrier repair, which is a different job from being non-comedogenic.
