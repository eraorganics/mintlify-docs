## What is skin barrier dysfunction?

Skin barrier dysfunction is a breakdown in the skin's outer defensive layer, the stratum corneum, so it can no longer hold water in or keep irritants out. It sits at the center of eczema. A review of [filaggrin and the skin barrier in atopic dermatitis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37758055/) explains how the outermost layer normally resists environmental threats and maintains balance, and how that resilience fails in eczema-prone skin.

When the barrier leaks, water escapes and allergens and microbes get in, which sparks the inflammation and itch of a flare. Understanding that leak is the key to caring for the condition.

## The genetics and mechanics of a leaky barrier

Part of the story is genetic. A review of [atopic dermatitis pathophysiology](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38724781/) describes how loss-of-function mutations in filaggrin raise water loss through the skin, shift its pH, and leave it dehydrated, all hallmarks of a dysfunctional barrier.

That is why care for eczema-prone skin leans so heavily on rebuilding the surface rather than only fighting inflammation. Restoring lipids and water supports [barrier function improvement](/products/baby-eczema-superbalm/barrier-function-improvement), and it is the reasoning behind emollient-rich balms for daily use.
