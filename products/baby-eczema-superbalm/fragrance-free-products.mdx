## What does fragrance-free mean?

Fragrance-free products are a category of hypoallergenic products: they contain no added fragrance, neither synthetic perfume nor masking scent. People often call them unscented products or perfume-free formulations, and for practical purposes the labels overlap, though there is one important distinction covered further down. What unites them is a deliberate choice to leave scent chemicals out of the formula so the skin never has to meet them.

That matters because fragrance is one of the most common triggers of skin reactions. A review of [fragrance allergic contact dermatitis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25207685/) describes fragrances as a leading cause of allergic contact dermatitis, capable of provoking reactions that range from hives to widespread rashes. On eczema-prone or infant skin, where the barrier is already compromised, avoiding fragrance removes a needless source of irritation. Era Organics' Baby Eczema Superbalm is formulated fragrance-free for exactly that reason, which is part of why it can serve as a fragrance-free eczema treatment.

## The attributes of a fragrance-free formula

A few measurable attributes separate a fragrance-free product from a scented one. The clearest is fragrance additive content, which in a true fragrance-free formula is zero: no perfume, no masking agent, nothing added purely for smell. A second is volatile organic compound emission, which tends to be minimal because most of the volatile scent molecules that evaporate off a scented product simply are not present. The third is allergen potential, which is reduced, since fragrance is such a frequent sensitizer that removing it takes a whole class of reactions off the table.

The contrast makes the point. Scented products and fragranced formulations deliberately add those volatile compounds for the sake of smell, and each one is a molecule the skin can react to. Fragrance is not one ingredient but a blend that can include dozens of compounds, and a person can react to any of them. Broader work on [allergic contact dermatitis from cosmetics](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24656778/) highlights fragrance among the most frequent culprits in everyday products, which is why sensitive-skin lines strip it out.

There is a labeling nuance worth knowing when you shop. Unscented products and perfume-free formulations sound identical to fragrance-free, but the words are not always used the same way. A truly fragrance-free product adds nothing for scent at all. An unscented product, by contrast, sometimes contains a masking fragrance whose only job is to neutralize the natural smell of the other ingredients, which means a reactive skin can still meet a scent chemical it did not expect. Reading the ingredient list, rather than the front-of-pack word, is the reliable way to tell them apart. Look for the absence of the catch-all term fragrance or parfum near the end of the list.

For an eczema-prone or infant barrier, that distinction is not academic. Skin that is already inflamed reacts more readily, so a hidden masking scent can undo the point of choosing a gentle product. Keeping fragrance additive content at zero, holding volatile compound emission low, and cutting a whole class of common allergens is why a fragrance-free eczema treatment is a sensible default for daily care on sensitive skin.
