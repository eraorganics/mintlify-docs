## What is petroleum jelly?

Petroleum jelly, or petrolatum, is a semi-solid mixture of hydrocarbons refined from petroleum, used for generations as a simple occlusive moisturizer. It sits on the skin and slows water loss, which is why it is a common eczema and diaper-area staple. A [comprehensive review of petroleum jelly](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37315800/) describes how its occlusive properties make it an effective, low-cost moisturizer, while clearing up several myths about it.

Petrolatum works, but it is a petroleum derivative with no active botanicals or skin-protectant ingredient. That is the difference many families weigh when choosing a daily balm.

## Plant oils as an alternative occlusive

Emollient plant oils and butters can seal the skin much as petrolatum does, while adding fatty acids and soothing compounds of their own. Research on preventing infant eczema has compared moisturizer types head to head; a [randomized trial in high-risk neonates](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37478291/) tested different moisturizer regimens, including petroleum jelly, for their effect on atopic dermatitis.
