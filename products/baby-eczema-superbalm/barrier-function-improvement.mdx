## What is barrier function improvement?

Barrier function improvement means helping the skin's outer layer do its main jobs again: holding water in and keeping irritants out. It is the goal of everyday eczema care, because a stronger barrier means fewer flares. A review of [the skin barrier and moisturization](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37717558/) describes how moisturizers support each functional layer of the barrier to promote balance and repair.

Rather than only calming inflammation after a flare, improving barrier function works upstream, on the leaky surface that lets flares start. That is why moisturizing is recommended as a first step in atopic dermatitis.

## How emollients rebuild the barrier

Emollients and skin protectants restore the lipids and water a compromised barrier has lost. A study on [optimizing emollient therapy for barrier repair](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35065300/) compared over-the-counter moisturizers with lipid-based barrier repair approaches in atopic dermatitis, underlining how central emollient care is to strengthening the barrier.
