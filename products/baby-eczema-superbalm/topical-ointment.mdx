## What is a topical ointment?

A topical ointment is a dermatological product, a semi-solid preparation applied externally to the skin for therapeutic purposes. It is also called a skin ointment, a dermatological ointment, or more broadly a topical preparation, and those terms refer to the same kind of format. Its defining attributes are physical: a viscosity that is semi-solid, thicker than a lotion but spreadable; a high oil content, typically a water-in-oil emulsion heavy on oils and waxes; and a penetration depth aimed at the epidermal layer, so it works at the surface rather than deep in the body. Era Organics made its Baby Eczema Superbalm an ointment on purpose, because that format suits damaged, eczema-prone skin. As an FDA-cleared OTC eczema treatment, it pairs that protective base with a 1% colloidal oatmeal active.

The oil-rich, semi-solid nature is the practical difference. A lotion is mostly water and absorbs fast; an ointment stays on the surface and seals it. This also sets it apart from an oral medication that is swallowed or an injectable treatment delivered into the body. A topical preparation acts where you put it, on the affected skin, which for a broken barrier losing moisture is exactly the point.

## Why an eczema topical ointment suits fragile skin

An eczema topical ointment is well matched to skin that leaks water and lets irritants in. Its oil-and-wax base and semi-solid viscosity slow that water loss and shield the skin from friction and allergens. Because it holds a high oil content and sits on the epidermal surface, it does the barrier's job while the barrier heals, unlike a thin, fast-absorbing lotion.

## Frequently asked questions about topical ointments

### What is the difference between an ointment and a cream?

An ointment has more oils and waxes and a semi-solid viscosity, so it stays on the surface as a protective layer; a cream holds more water and absorbs faster. For eczema, an ointment's staying power seals the barrier better. Superbalm is an ointment for that reason.

### Is a topical ointment greasy?

A skin ointment feels richer than a lotion because of its high oil content, but a thin layer absorbs into the top of the skin and the rest forms a protective seal. Using a small amount on damp skin keeps it comfortable. The payoff is longer-lasting barrier protection.

### Can I use an eczema ointment on a baby?

Superbalm is made for the whole family, including babies, and its ointment base is gentle. Because it is a topical preparation applied to the surface rather than an oral medication, patch test first and check with your pediatrician. Apply a thin layer to the affected area.
