## What is a topical ointment?

A topical ointment is a thick, oil-based product applied to the skin. It holds more oils and waxes than a lotion or cream, so it forms a lasting protective layer. Era Organics made its Baby Eczema Superbalm an ointment on purpose — the format suits damaged, eczema-prone skin. As an FDA-cleared OTC eczema treatment, it pairs that protective base with a 1% colloidal oatmeal active.

Texture is the practical difference. A lotion is mostly water and absorbs fast; an ointment stays on the surface and seals it. For a broken barrier losing moisture, staying on the surface is the point.

## Why an ointment suits eczema-prone skin

Eczema skin leaks water and lets irritants in. An ointment's oil-and-wax base slows that water loss and shields the skin from friction and allergens. It does the barrier's job while the barrier heals.

That occlusive protection is why an ointment outperforms a thin lotion on flaring skin. An ointment goes further than it looks. A thin layer spreads over the skin and lasts, so a little covers a lot. It works best applied to slightly damp skin to lock in moisture.

## Frequently asked questions about topical ointments

### What is the difference between an ointment and a cream?

An ointment has more oils and waxes and stays on the surface as a protective layer; a cream holds more water and absorbs faster. For eczema, an ointment's staying power seals the barrier better. Superbalm is an ointment for that reason.

### Is an ointment greasy?

An ointment feels richer than a lotion, but a thin layer absorbs into the top of the skin and the rest forms a protective seal. Using a small amount on damp skin keeps it comfortable. The payoff is longer-lasting barrier protection.

### Can I use an eczema ointment on a baby?

Superbalm is made for the whole family, including babies, and its ointment base is gentle. As with any new product on an infant, patch test first and check with your pediatrician. Apply a thin layer to the affected area.
