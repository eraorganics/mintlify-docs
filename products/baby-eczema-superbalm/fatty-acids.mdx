## What are fatty acids in skincare?

Fatty acids are the building-block lipids in plant oils, and they are part of what keeps the skin barrier intact and holding water. Linoleic acid, an essential omega-6 fatty acid, is especially important to healthy skin. A review of [linoleic acid in skin and hair health](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39796110/) describes how it is incorporated into the complex lipids of the skin barrier and supports its function.

Because the body cannot make essential fatty acids, they have to come from the diet or, topically, from the oils in a balm. That is one reason plant-oil formulas matter for eczema-prone skin.

## How fatty acids support barrier repair

A leaky, eczema-prone barrier is often short on the very lipids fatty acids help supply, so replenishing them supports repair. A review of [natural oils for skin-barrier repair](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28707186/) notes that plant oils rich in these fatty acids carry anti-inflammatory and anti-itch properties on top of their moisturizing effect.
