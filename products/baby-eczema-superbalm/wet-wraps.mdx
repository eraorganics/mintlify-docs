## What is wet-wrap therapy?

Wet-wrap therapy is a technique for calming stubborn eczema: moisturizer is applied, then a damp layer of clothing or bandage is covered by a dry layer, sealing hydration against the skin. It is used for severe or flaring atopic dermatitis, often in children. A [practical guideline on wet-wrap treatment in children](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22256990/) describes it as a relatively safe and effective intervention for severe or refractory eczema.

The wraps themselves are just cloth; the relief comes from the moisturizer trapped underneath. That makes the choice of emollient part of how well the technique works.

## How wet wraps amplify a balm

By holding a moisturizer against the skin under an occlusive layer, wet wraps boost hydration and cool the itch during a flare. Major eczema guidance reflects this; the 2023 [atopic dermatitis guidelines](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38108679/) address wet-wrap therapy among the options for managing more difficult disease, typically layered over topical care.

## Frequently asked questions about wet wraps

### Do wet wraps help eczema?

Wet-wrap therapy is a recognized option for severe or flaring eczema, sealing moisturizer against the skin to boost hydration and calm itch. Work with a clinician on how and when to use it, especially for children.

### Can I use Superbalm for wet wraps?

Superbalm can serve as the emollient layer under wet wraps, applied before the damp and dry layers. Follow your clinician's protocol, since some wrap routines also include a prescribed medicated cream.

### Are wet wraps safe for babies?

Wet wraps can be used in children but should be guided by a pediatrician or dermatologist, as improper use carries risks. Get a plan tailored to your child before starting.
