## What is wet-wrap therapy?

Wet-wrap therapy is a form of occlusive therapy for calming stubborn eczema: moisturizer is applied, then a damp layer of clothing or bandage is covered by a dry layer, sealing hydration against the skin. The wet layers go by several names, including occlusive dressings, topical wet dressings, and plain wet dressings, and they all describe the same damp-over-emollient approach. It is used for severe or flaring atopic dermatitis, often in children. A [practical guideline on wet-wrap treatment in children](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22256990/) describes it as a relatively safe and effective intervention for severe or refractory eczema.

A few attributes define how the wraps are set up. The material type is usually soft cloth, cotton or gauze, cut to fit the affected area. The moisture level of the inner layer is damp, not soaking, so it holds hydration without dripping. And the application duration typically runs a couple of hours up to around eight, often overnight, before the wraps are removed. The wraps themselves are just cloth; the relief comes from the moisturizer trapped underneath, which makes the choice of emollient part of how well the technique works.

## How the wet wraps technique amplifies a balm

The wet wraps technique works by holding a moisturizer against the skin under an occlusive layer, boosting hydration and cooling the itch during a flare. That is the opposite of dry application or open-air treatment, where the emollient sits exposed and its effect fades faster; the damp seal is what makes the difference. Major eczema guidance reflects this; the 2023 [atopic dermatitis guidelines](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38108679/) address wet-wrap therapy among the options for managing more difficult disease, typically layered over topical care.

A gentle, fragrance-free balm is well suited to the emollient layer beneath the wet dressings. The wraps work partly by cooling: as water in the damp cloth evaporates slowly, it draws heat off inflamed skin, which is a large part of why the itch eases during a session. They also form a physical barrier that stops a child from scratching the skin raw, giving the surface a protected window to calm down. ## Frequently asked questions about wet wraps

### Do eczema wet wraps help?

Eczema wet wraps are a recognized occlusive therapy for severe or flaring eczema, sealing moisturizer against the skin to boost hydration and calm itch. Work with a clinician on how and when to use them, especially for children.

### Can I use Superbalm for wet wraps?

Superbalm can serve as the emollient layer under wet dressings, applied before the damp and dry layers. Follow your clinician's protocol, since some wrap routines also include a prescribed medicated cream.

### Are wet wraps safe for babies?

Wet wraps can be used in children but should be guided by a pediatrician or dermatologist, as improper use carries risks. Damp material left on too long is not without downside, so get a plan tailored to your child, including the right duration, before starting.
