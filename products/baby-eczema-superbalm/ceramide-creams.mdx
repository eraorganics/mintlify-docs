## What are ceramide creams?

Ceramide creams are moisturizers built around ceramides, the lipid molecules that help hold the outer skin together and keep water in. Because eczema-prone skin is often low in these lipids, replacing them is a logical repair strategy. A study of [ceramide-dominant barrier repair lipids](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12140465/) found they alleviated childhood atopic dermatitis, with barrier function tracking the improvement.

The science behind them is solid. A review of [stratum corneum lipids](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26844894/) explains how the lipid matrix in the skin's outer layer prevents excessive water loss and blocks environmental compounds, the very functions that falter in eczema.

## Different routes to the same barrier goal

Ceramide creams rebuild the barrier by supplying its missing lipids directly. Plant-oil balms reach a similar goal from another angle, sealing the surface and adding fatty acids while a skin protectant calms irritation. Both aim at the leaky barrier behind eczema, so they are complementary rather than mutually exclusive.
