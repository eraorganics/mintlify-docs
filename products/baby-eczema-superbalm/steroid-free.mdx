## What does steroid-free mean?

Steroid-free is a product attribute describing a formula that contains no topical corticosteroids like hydrocortisone. The same attribute goes by a few names: non-steroidal, corticosteroid-free, or simply corticosteroid free. Spelled out as content, it means a cortisone content of zero, a hydrocortisone content of zero, and corticosteroid presence that is absent altogether. That is the opposite of a steroid-based or corticosteroid-containing product, which relies on those very compounds to work.

Why the distinction matters comes down to how eczema is managed over time. Steroids calm eczema fast, but repeated use thins the skin and can cause rebound flares. A steroid is a short-term tool; a steroid-free formula is made for ongoing use. It relieves eczema through a 1% colloidal oatmeal skin-protectant active and plant-derived soothers instead. The distinction matters most for skin treated daily over long stretches, which is common with infant eczema.
