## What does FDA-cleared OTC mean?

FDA-cleared OTC means a product is a recognized over-the-counter drug, not just a cosmetic. It works under an FDA monograph — a public standard that sets an approved active ingredient, use, and labeling. Era Organics' Baby Eczema Superbalm qualifies as an OTC eczema treatment because its active, colloidal oatmeal at 1%, is an FDA-recognized skin protectant. That status is why the balm can state it treats eczema, where a cosmetic cannot.

The difference is legal, not marketing. A cosmetic may only "soothe" or "moisturize"; an OTC drug with a monograph active may state a treatment use within its label. Superbalm sits in the second group.

## What the Drug Facts label allows

An OTC drug carries a Drug Facts panel that fixes exactly what it may claim. Superbalm's active is colloidal oatmeal 1%, a skin protectant. Its labeled use is to temporarily protect and help relieve minor skin irritation and itching due to eczema and rashes.

Those are the boundaries. The balm can speak to eczema, itch, and rashes because the label says so, and it stays softer on other uses. The colloidal oatmeal active behind that clearance is covered in [colloidal oatmeal](/ingredients/gluten-free-oats).

## Frequently asked questions about FDA-cleared OTC status

### Is Superbalm an FDA-approved drug?

Superbalm is an FDA-cleared OTC eczema treatment, meaning it follows the FDA monograph for its colloidal oatmeal skin-protectant active. That is different from a prescription drug's individual FDA approval. It is available over the counter, without a prescription.

### Why can it claim to treat eczema when other creams cannot?

Because colloidal oatmeal is an FDA-recognized OTC skin protectant with an eczema use on its monograph. That lets the Drug Facts label state relief of itching and irritation due to eczema and rashes. Cosmetics without a monograph active cannot make that claim.

### Does OTC mean it is safe for babies?

OTC status speaks to the drug label, not to any single user. Superbalm is made for the whole family and is gentle by design, but for an infant, follow your pediatrician's guidance. Patch test any new product first.
