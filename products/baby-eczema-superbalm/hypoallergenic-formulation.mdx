## What does hypoallergenic mean?

Hypoallergenic is meant to signal that a product is formulated to be less likely to cause an allergic reaction. The catch is that the term is not tightly regulated, so it does not guarantee a product is allergen-free. Researchers have even tested the claim directly; an [assessment of the hypoallergenicity of ten skincare products](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24595569/) examined how well such products actually serve sensitive skin.

Because the word promises more than it can deliver, Era Organics prefers to describe what its Baby Eczema Superbalm actually contains and leaves out, rather than lean on a hypoallergenic label.

## What Superbalm removes, and what it keeps

Superbalm strips out many of the usual triggers: it is fragrance-free, dye-free, paraben-free, and free of harsh preservatives and drying alcohol. That removes some of the most common causes of reactions. But natural ingredients can still be allergens, and honesty matters here.

The balm contains coconut, which the FDA classifies as a tree nut, and chamomile, a member of the Compositae (ragweed) family. Compositae plants are a recognized cause of allergic contact dermatitis; a study of [contact allergy to Compositae plants in atopic dermatitis patients](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15503788/) found meaningful sensitivity in that group. So while Superbalm removes many irritants, it is not allergen-free, and we do not label it hypoallergenic. This is the same guidance covered in the balm's [dermatological safety](/products/baby-eczema-superbalm/dermatological-safety) notes.

## Using Superbalm safely with allergies in mind

For most families the removed irritants make Superbalm a gentle daily choice, and its emollient plant oils are chosen for delicate skin. If your child has a known coconut, tree-nut, or ragweed-family allergy, take extra care: patch test a small area first, and check with your pediatrician or allergist before regular use. The balm is also [fragrance-free](/products/baby-eczema-superbalm/fragrance-free-products), which removes another frequent trigger.

## Frequently asked questions about hypoallergenic formulation

### Is Superbalm hypoallergenic?

We do not claim it is. Superbalm removes many common irritants, but it contains coconut (an FDA-classified tree nut) and chamomile (a ragweed-family botanical), so it is not allergen-free. Families with those allergies should patch test and consult an allergist.

### Why avoid the word hypoallergenic?

The term is not strictly regulated and cannot guarantee no reaction. We would rather list what the balm removes and what it contains so you can judge it for your own skin.

### How do I know if my child will react?

Patch test a small amount on the inner forearm and watch for a day before wider use, and stop if the skin reacts. Involve your pediatrician or allergist if there is a known allergy.
