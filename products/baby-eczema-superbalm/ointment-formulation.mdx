## What is ointment formulation?

Ointment formulation is the way an oil-and-wax base is built and the active ingredients are blended into it. The base decides how the product feels, how well it seals the skin, and how few extra ingredients it needs. Era Organics formulates its Baby Eczema Superbalm as an ointment so the base itself does protective work. The FDA-cleared OTC treatment carries a 1% colloidal oatmeal active through that base.

The choice of base is the first decision. A water-heavy cream needs emulsifiers and preservatives to stay stable; an oil-and-wax ointment needs far fewer. Fewer added ingredients means fewer potential irritants.

## How Superbalm's base is built

Superbalm's base combines plant butters, oils, and wax. Cocoa butter and coconut oil give it body and emollient richness; [beeswax](/ingredients/beeswax) sets the structure and seals the surface. Plant soothers and the oatmeal active are carried within that base.

Because the base is oil-and-wax rather than a water emulsion, Superbalm needs no synthetic preservatives, and it stays steroid-free, fragrance-free, and petroleum-free. The emollients that make up the base are covered in [coconut oil](/ingredients/coconut-oil).

## Why the formulation matters for eczema

A formula for eczema-prone skin has to protect without provoking. Every ingredient is a possible trigger, so a short, oil-based recipe lowers the risk while sealing the barrier. The base is doing double duty — protection and delivery.

Superbalm's formulation keeps the ingredient list to plant-derived actives and emollients around the colloidal oatmeal active. That restraint is what makes it suitable for daily, whole-family use. For the full formula, see the [Baby Eczema Superbalm](/products/baby-eczema-superbalm) reference.

## Frequently asked questions about ointment formulation

### Why does an ointment need fewer preservatives?

Water in a formula invites microbial growth, so water-based creams need preservatives. An oil-and-wax ointment holds little to no water, so it needs far fewer. That lets Superbalm keep a short, gentle ingredient list.

### What makes up the Superbalm base?

Plant butters and oils like cocoa butter and coconut oil provide the emollient body, and beeswax sets the structure and seals the surface. The colloidal oatmeal active and plant soothers are blended into that base. It contains no petroleum or synthetic fragrance.

### Does the formulation affect how it feels?

Yes. An oil-and-wax base feels richer than a lotion and stays on the surface longer. A thin layer absorbs at the top while the rest forms a protective seal. That staying power is the goal for a barrier-repair balm.
