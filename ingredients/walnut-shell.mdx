Walnut shell powder (Juglans Regia Shell Powder) is a physical exfoliant derived from finely milled walnut shells that buffs away dead skin cells, surface debris, and pore-clogging buildup without the sharp edges of synthetic microbeads. The particles are irregular in shape but rounded enough at cosmetic grades to lift dullness through mechanical friction rather than cutting into the skin surface.

Physical exfoliants work by disrupting the outermost layer of the outer skin layer (stratum corneum), loosening the bonds that hold dead dead skin cells (corneocytes) in place so rinsing removes them. Walnut shell powder achieves this without synthetic abrasives, which is why the Revive Face Scrub uses it as the primary exfoliating agent rather than plastic microbeads — now banned in rinse-off products across many markets due to environmental concerns.

The Microdermabrasion Scrub pairs walnut shell with aloe vera (Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice) and manuka honey (Leptospermum Scoparium Mel), so the skin receives conditioning alongside the mechanical exfoliation. Walnut shell on its own removes buildup and smooths texture; the surrounding formula addresses any dryness the exfoliation step introduces. Research into medical-grade manuka honey has documented its role in chronic wound resolution with no side effects across several months of application (White et al., British Journal of Nursing, 2019), supporting its broader reputation as a skin-conditioning agent. The Gentle Face Exfoliant is designed to be used 2–3 times per week, starting at 1–2 times for sensitive skin, so walnut shell delivers progressive texture refinement without overworking the barrier.

Patch test on the inner arm before first use, and avoid the eye area during application.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you heal micro-tears in your skin?

Micro-tears are not something a product fixes — skin resolves minor surface disruption on its own through its natural renewal cycle. Your role is to stop causing them and support skin while it recovers: pause exfoliating for a stretch, keep the area moisturized, and avoid anything gritty or stripping until skin feels calm again.

The more useful question is how to exfoliate without creating them in the first place. The micro-tear reputation attached to walnut scrubs comes from coarse, sharp-edged grit. Cosmetic-grade walnut shell powder (Juglans Regia Shell Powder) is finely milled, with particles rounded enough to buff away dead skin cells through gentle friction rather than cutting into the skin surface. In the Revive Microdermabrasion Scrub, that powder sits in a base of aloe vera and manuka honey, so skin receives conditioning alongside the mechanical exfoliation and the formula addresses any dryness the step introduces. Research into medical-grade manuka honey has documented its role in chronic wound resolution with no side effects across several months of application (White et al., British Journal of Nursing, 2019), which supports its broader reputation as a skin-conditioning agent. Keep use to 2–3 times per week — 1–2 for sensitive skin — patch test on your inner arm first, and avoid the eye area.

### Is a walnut scrub good for dry skin?

A walnut scrub can work well for dry skin, provided the walnut shell is finely milled and the formula gives something back to the skin afterward. Dry skin tends to hold onto dead skin cells, and that buildup reads as flakiness and dullness. Walnut shell powder (Juglans Regia Shell Powder) is a physical exfoliant that buffs away those dead cells and surface debris, loosening the bonds that hold them in place so they rinse off — lifting dullness through gentle mechanical friction rather than sharp edges.

The common worry with walnut scrubs is grit. At cosmetic grades, the particles are rounded enough to polish rather than cut, which is why the Revive Microdermabrasion Scrub uses walnut shell as its exfoliating agent instead of plastic microbeads, now banned in rinse-off products across many markets. Just as important for dry skin is what surrounds the walnut: the scrub pairs it with aloe vera and manuka honey, so the skin gets conditioning alongside the exfoliation, and the formula addresses any dryness the exfoliating step introduces.

Use it 2–3 times per week — starting at 1–2 times if your skin is sensitive — patch test on the inner arm before first use, and keep it away from the eye area.
