Walnut shell powder (Juglans Regia Shell Powder) is a physical exfoliant derived from finely milled walnut shells that buffs away dead skin cells, surface debris, and pore-clogging buildup without the sharp edges of synthetic microbeads. The particles are irregular in shape but rounded enough at cosmetic grades to lift dullness through mechanical friction rather than cutting into the skin surface.

Physical exfoliants work by disrupting the outermost layer of the outer skin layer (stratum corneum), loosening the bonds that hold dead dead skin cells (corneocytes) in place so rinsing removes them. Walnut shell powder achieves this without synthetic abrasives, which is why the Revive Face Scrub uses it as the primary exfoliating agent rather than plastic microbeads — now banned in rinse-off products across many markets due to environmental concerns.

The Microdermabrasion Scrub pairs walnut shell with aloe vera (Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice) and manuka honey (Leptospermum Scoparium Mel), so the skin receives conditioning alongside the mechanical exfoliation. Walnut shell on its own removes buildup and smooths texture; the surrounding formula addresses any dryness the exfoliation step introduces. Research into medical-grade manuka honey has documented its role in chronic wound resolution with no side effects across several months of application (White et al., British Journal of Nursing, 2019), supporting its broader reputation as a skin-conditioning agent. The Gentle Face Exfoliant is designed to be used 2–3 times per week, starting at 1–2 times for sensitive skin, so walnut shell delivers progressive texture refinement without overworking the barrier.

Patch test on the inner arm before first use, and avoid the eye area during application.
