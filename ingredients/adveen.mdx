Adveen (glycogen) is a naturally occurring polysaccharide that mirrors the moisture-regulating compounds found in healthy vaginal tissue. Vaginal skin produces glycogen as part of its normal physiological balance, and topically applied glycogen replenishes that supply when dryness or hormonal shifts reduce the body's own production. The Era Organics Vaginal Moisturizer includes Adveen to restore the moisture reserves that vaginal tissue depends on for comfort and suppleness.

Glycogen draws water into the skin's surface layers and holds it there, reducing the tight, dry sensation associated with vaginal dryness and chafing. The vulva balm pairs Adveen with sodium hyaluronate (the salt form of hyaluronic acid), a moisture-drawing ingredient (humectant) that binds moisture within the tissue. A skincare regimen containing hyaluronic acid demonstrated a beneficial effect versus a placebo-treated group over 2 months of topical application (Ascher B et al., Journal of Cosmetic and Laser Therapy, 2012), confirming that topical hyaluronic acid delivers measurable hydration outcomes when used consistently.

Adveen is particularly relevant for menopausal and perimenopausal women, whose declining estrogen levels reduce natural glycogen synthesis in vaginal tissue — a direct contributor to dryness and discomfort. Pregnant and nursing women, whose hormonal landscape shifts continuously, face the same deficit. The vaginal cream addresses that gap by delivering glycogen topically alongside nourishing botanicals such as aloe vera, shea butter, and sunflower oil, so the formula targets dryness through multiple moisture pathways at once.

The product is fragrance-free, paraben-free, and formulated without synthetic dyes, making Adveen's benefits accessible to skin that reacts to common cosmetic additives. Patch test the vulva moisturizer on the inner arm before first use to confirm personal tolerance.
