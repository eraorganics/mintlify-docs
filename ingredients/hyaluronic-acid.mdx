Hyaluronic acid (sodium hyaluronate in its salt form) is a water-binding molecule that draws moisture into skin tissue and holds it there, making it a natural fit for a vaginal moisturizer designed to address dryness and discomfort in the vulvar area.

Vaginal and vulvar skin loses moisture faster than skin on other parts of the body, particularly during perimenopause, postpartum recovery, and hormonal shifts that reduce natural lubrication. Hyaluronic acid attracts water from the surrounding environment and from deeper skin layers, depositing that moisture directly into the outer skin layer (stratum corneum) where dryness originates. The Era Organics Certified Vaginal Moisturizer delivers sodium hyaluronate alongside organic plant oils and shea butter so the moisture-drawing ingredient (humectant) effect is locked in rather than lost to evaporation.

The safety record for hyaluronic acid on sensitive tissue is well-established. Clinical and biocompatibility data spanning longer than 15 years support the favorable clinical safety profile of hyaluronic acid and its degradation products, with researchers concluding that "physicians should be confident in offering these products" based on the strength of the empirical evidence (De Boulle K et al., Dermatologic Surgery, 2013). That record makes it an appropriate ingredient for a formula intended for daily use on the vulva, where skin tolerability is a primary concern.

The vaginal balm pairs sodium hyaluronate with sunflower oil, coconut oil, sweet almond oil, and beeswax to create a layered moisture strategy: the hyaluronic acid draws water in, and the lipid-rich oils and wax seal it against the skin surface. Patch test before first use by applying a small amount to the inner arm to confirm tolerance before applying to vaginal tissue.
