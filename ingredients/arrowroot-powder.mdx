Arrowroot powder (derived from the tropical plant *Maranta arundinacea*) is a fine, starch-based absorbent that draws moisture away from the skin's surface without clogging pores or leaving a heavy residue. The powder sits at the foundation of the Era Organics Talc-Free formula because it replicates the moisture-absorbing function of talc while carrying none of talc's associated safety concerns.

Arrowroot powder absorbs sweat and humidity on contact, keeping the diaper area, skin folds, and underarms dry between changes. Dry skin surfaces experience significantly less friction, which directly reduces chafing in areas where skin presses against skin or fabric. The Organic Baby Powder uses arrowroot as its primary absorbent base precisely because this mechanism operates through physical adsorption rather than chemical activity, making it suitable for frequent application on newborns, infants, and adults alike.

The particle texture of arrowroot powder is finer than cornstarch, so the Natural Baby Powder spreads with a lighter, silkier feel against delicate skin. Paired with Cornstarch and Kaolin Clay in the Chafing Prevention Powder formula, arrowroot powder creates a layered absorbency system, each ingredient drawing moisture at a slightly different rate and particle size, so the combined powder stays effective longer than any single absorbent alone.

Moist, occluded skin folds are a documented environment for secondary bacterial and fungal infections associated with intertrigo (Janniger et al., American Family Physician, 2005). Keeping those zones consistently dry is the first line of defense against that cascade, and arrowroot powder's moisture-wicking capacity is the mechanism through which the Baby Dusting Powder addresses that concern. Patch test a small area before first use, and pat the powder lightly onto skin rather than shaking a heavy cloud near a baby's face.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is arrowroot powder safe to use on skin (including the face)?

Yes — arrowroot powder is a fine, starch-based absorbent that draws moisture away from the skin's surface through simple physical adsorption rather than chemical activity, without clogging pores or leaving a heavy residue, which makes it gentle enough for frequent use on newborns, infants, and adults alike. That gentleness is why it forms the absorbent base of Era Organics' Talc-Free Baby Powder, where it replicates talc's moisture-absorbing job with none of talc's associated safety concerns. On the face, apply it with care: patch test a small area before first use, and pat the powder lightly onto skin instead of shaking a cloud of it into the air — especially near a baby's face.

### Is arrowroot powder the same as cornstarch, and which is better?

No — they're different starches. Arrowroot powder comes from the tropical plant *Maranta arundinacea*, and its particle texture is finer than cornstarch, so it spreads with a lighter, silkier feel on delicate skin. Both absorb moisture well, which is why Era Organics Talc-Free Baby Powder uses them together rather than choosing one: arrowroot, cornstarch, and kaolin clay each draw moisture at a slightly different rate and particle size, so the combined powder stays effective longer than any single absorbent alone.

### What does arrowroot powder do for skin?

Arrowroot powder — a fine starch from the tropical Maranta arundinacea plant — absorbs sweat and moisture on contact without clogging pores or leaving a heavy residue. Because it works through physical absorption rather than chemical activity, it helps keep the diaper area, skin folds, and underarms dry, and drier skin experiences less friction and chafing. Its particles are finer than cornstarch, giving it a lighter, silkier feel on delicate skin, which is why Era Organics' Talc-Free Baby Powder uses arrowroot as its primary absorbent base alongside cornstarch and kaolin clay — replicating talc's moisture-absorbing function without talc's safety concerns. Patch test a small area before first use, and pat the powder on gently rather than shaking a cloud near a baby's face.
