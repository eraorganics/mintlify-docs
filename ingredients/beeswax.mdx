Beeswax (cera alba) is a natural occlusive wax that forms a breathable, water-resistant layer on the skin's surface, locking in moisture without suffocating the skin. Unlike petrolatum-based moisture-sealing ingredients (occlusives), beeswax contains naturally occurring long-chain esters that give it a semi-permeable structure, allowing the skin to exchange gases while still reducing moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss). Superbalm uses beeswax to hold its oil-based ingredients, Calendula Oil, Coconut Oil, and Avocado Oil, in a stable, spreadable ointment texture that stays on irritated or eczema-prone skin rather than absorbing immediately.

Beeswax gives Era Organics Superbalm healing ointment its protective film over rash-prone or chafed areas, including the diaper zone and dry patches on the elbows and knees. The wax matrix slows the evaporation of the superbalm plant-based skin-softening ingredients (emollients) underneath, extending their contact time with the outer skin layer (stratum corneum). Patch testing before first use is advisable for ragweed-sensitive users, since cross-reactivity between propolis (a related bee product) and beeswax exists, though it is rare: in a 2,828-patient multicenter patch-test survey, only 4 of 55 propolis-allergic subjects (7.2%) showed a confirmed reaction to beeswax (Rajpara et al., Contact Dermatitis, 2009).

The balm's beeswax base has demonstrated real-world tolerability alongside Calendula Oil in formulated herbal products. A 59-patient study assessed a combination formula containing beeswax and Calendula oil used daily during radiotherapy and for two weeks after treatment, recording tolerability across the full application period (Koukourakis et al., The International Journal of Lower Extremity Wounds, 2022). Superbalm pairs these two ingredients in the same ointment, applying the same occlusive-plus-botanical logic to everyday skin concerns like dryness, irritation, and diaper rash.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is beeswax good for your skin?

Beeswax (cera alba) is a useful skincare ingredient partly because of what it doesn't do: it doesn't seal the skin completely. Its long-chain esters create a semi-permeable, water-resistant layer that locks in moisture while still allowing the skin to exchange gases — unlike petrolatum, which is fully occlusive.

That structure makes beeswax well-suited as an ointment base. In Era Organics Superbalm, beeswax holds Calendula Oil, Coconut Oil, and Avocado Oil in a stable, spreadable formula that stays on the skin rather than absorbing immediately. The wax slows evaporation of the emollients underneath, extending their contact time with the outer skin layer (stratum corneum). That makes Superbalm effective for soothing eczema and atopic dermatitis, and for calming dry patches on elbows and knees, chafed skin, and the diaper zone.

One sensitivity note: a 2,828-patient multicenter patch-test survey found only 7.2% of propolis-allergic subjects reacted to beeswax — cross-reactivity is rare, but if you have ragweed sensitivities, a patch test before first use is a reasonable precaution.

### Is beeswax safe for babies?

Beeswax (cera alba) is generally well-tolerated on infant skin. Unlike petrolatum, it has a semi-permeable structure built from long-chain esters that seals in moisture while still allowing the skin to breathe — an important distinction for sensitive baby skin. It works as an occlusive, reducing transepidermal water loss and forming a protective film over rash-prone areas like the diaper zone, dry elbows, and knees.

The main safety consideration is allergy. Beeswax can cross-react with propolis in sensitive individuals, though this is uncommon: a 2,828-patient patch-test survey found that only 7.2% of propolis-allergic subjects reacted to beeswax (Rajpara et al., 2009). For ragweed-sensitive families, a patch test before first use is a reasonable precaution.
