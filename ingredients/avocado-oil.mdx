Avocado oil (pressed from the fruit of *Persea americana*) is a dense emollient that deposits fatty acids, fat-soluble vitamins, and plant sterols directly onto the skin's surface. Superbalm includes avocado oil because dry, inflamed skin, whether from eczema, cradle cap, or diaper rash, loses lipids faster than it replenishes them, and avocado oil's high oleic acid content replenishes that lipid layer immediately on contact.

The fatty acid profile of avocado oil is dominated by oleic acid, a monounsaturated fat that mirrors the skin's own intercellular lipids. Oleic acid penetrates the outer layer of the skin (stratum corneum) and softens the tight, thickened texture that forms over dry, eczema-prone patches. Canola oil's unsaponifiable lipid fraction assisted the skin in supplying the damaged barrier with adequate lipids, reducing SLS-induced irritation in barrier-compromised skin (Lodén et al., British Journal of Dermatology, 1996); avocado oil supplies the same oleic-rich unsaponifiable lipid class, applying that barrier-restoration mechanism to the healing ointment's formula.

Avocado oil's natural sterols, beta-sitosterol, campesterol, and stigmasterol, calm surface redness and soften rough texture without occluding pores or leaving a heavy residue. Beeswax in Baby Superbalm then seals the emollient layer in place, extending the window during which avocado oil's lipids absorb into compromised skin. The result is a two-stage action: avocado oil conditions and softens the skin first, and beeswax locks that moisture barrier in.

Patch test the balm on the inside of the elbow before first full-body use, particularly for infants with known sensitivities.

### Is avocado oil safe for babies?

Avocado oil is generally well-tolerated on baby skin and a common ingredient in gentle infant care formulas. Pressed from the fruit of Persea americana, it delivers oleic acid — a monounsaturated fat that mirrors the skin's own intercellular lipids — letting it penetrate the outer skin layer and soften dry, thickened patches on contact.

For babies with eczema or atopic dermatitis, this matters: inflamed skin loses its fatty-acid barrier faster than it rebuilds it, and avocado oil begins replenishing that layer immediately. The oil's natural plant sterols also calm surface redness and soften rough texture without clogging pores or leaving a heavy residue.
