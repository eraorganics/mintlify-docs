Carrot seed oil (Daucus carota sativa) is a botanical oil pressed from the seeds of the wild carrot plant, included in the Relief OTC Dermatitis Cream for its skin-nourishing and soothing properties. The oil is rich in beta-carotene and tocopherols, which are antioxidant compounds that protect the skin surface from oxidative stress — a factor that worsens dry, reactive, and rash-prone skin.

Carrot seed oil softens the outer skin layer and helps maintain suppleness in skin compromised by chronic dryness. For skin dealing with dermatitis or eczema, where the barrier is already disrupted, an emollient that buffers environmental stress without adding fragrance or synthetic dyes addresses a direct need. Era Organics Dermatitis Cream is fragrance-free and synthetic-dye-free, so carrot seed oil contributes its soothing character without common irritant carriers.

The oil pairs with other ingredients in the Eczema Relief Cream to address the broader burden of atopic skin. Atopic dermatitis is a prevalent inflammatory skin condition affecting both children and adults, with a global prevalence of 15–30% (Chaudhary et al., Journal of Biotechnology and Biomedicine, 2024). Within a formula targeting that population, each ingredient carries a defined role: carrot seed oil moisturizes and softens while the formula's colloidal oatmeal 1% provides FDA-recognized skin protectant coverage.

Era Organics Relief is designed for the whole family, including babies, so the choice to include carrot seed oil over harsher actives reflects a deliberate formulation approach. Patch testing before first use is the recommended practice, particularly for users with known sensitivities to Asteraceae-family botanicals, since carrot (Daucus carota) belongs to the Apiaceae family and is botanically distinct, but individual sensitivities vary and a patch test on a small area of skin before applying the OTC Dermatitis Moisturizer broadly is sound practice.
