Gluten free oats (Avena sativa) are a skin-soothing grain extract that calms itchy and inflamed skin without synthetic additives. The outer layer of the oat kernel contains avenanthramides, a class of polyphenolic compounds that reduce redness and relieve the itch cycle that characterizes atopic dermatitis and eczema flares. Oat extracts deposit a thin protective film across the skin surface, slowing moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss) and keeping dry, compromised skin from losing more moisture between applications.

Gluten free oats in the balm address a practical concern for families managing both eczema and gluten sensitivity. Standard oat-based formulas carry a cross-contamination risk that disqualifies them for reactive skin. Superbalm uses oats sourced and processed to minimize that exposure, making Superbalm accessible to a broader range of users without sacrificing its calming properties.

The soothing role of botanical ingredients on atopic skin has been evaluated in controlled settings. Canola oil's unsaponifiable lipids reduced surfactant-induced irritation in barrier-compromised skin by replenishing the damaged barrier with adequate lipids (Lodén et al., British Journal of Dermatology, 1996); oat-derived lipids and polysaccharides operate through the same barrier-restoration pathway, depositing occlusive and emollient compounds across the outer skin layer (stratum corneum).

Patch test the healing ointment on the inside of the elbow before first full application, especially for users with known ragweed or Asteraceae sensitivities.
