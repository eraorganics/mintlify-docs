Coconut oil is a medium-chain fatty acid–rich emollient that moisturizes dry, compromised skin by forming an occlusive barrier over the outer skin layer (stratum corneum). The predominant fatty acid in coconut oil is lauric acid, which binds to the skin surface and slows moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss). Superbalm includes coconut oil to keep moisture locked into delicate infant skin between diaper changes and bath times.

Virgin coconut oil applied overnight produced a 25% increase in perceived moisture content in human subjects (Saraogi et al., Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology, 2021). The healing ointment draws on this moisturizing capacity to address the dry, flaky patches associated with eczema, cradle cap, and diaper rash without relying on mineral oil, petrolatum derivatives, or synthetic skin-softening ingredients (emollients).

Coconut oil is a possible contact allergen for some individuals, particularly those with known sensitivities to coconut-derived ingredients (Aalto-Korte et al., Contact Dermatitis, 2014). Patch test the Superbalm healing ointment on the inside of the elbow before first full-body use, especially for newborns or anyone with a history of contact dermatitis.

### Is coconut oil good for a baby's skin?

Coconut oil can be a good moisturizer for baby skin when properly formulated. Its primary fatty acid, lauric acid, binds to the skin surface and slows moisture loss — which helps keep delicate infant skin hydrated between diaper changes and bath times.

Era Organics Superbalm uses coconut oil as part of a mineral-oil-free formula made for sensitive baby skin. As an OTC skin protectant, it helps relieve and soothe eczema and atopic dermatitis, and can also soothe the dry, flaky patches associated with cradle cap and diaper rash.

One important caveat: coconut oil is a potential contact allergen for some individuals, especially those with known coconut sensitivities. Before the first full-body use — and particularly on newborns — patch test Superbalm on the inside of the elbow and wait 24 hours before applying more broadly.

For babies with eczema or very sensitive skin, a tested OTC product like Superbalm provides coconut oil's moisturizing benefits without mineral oil or synthetic emollients.
