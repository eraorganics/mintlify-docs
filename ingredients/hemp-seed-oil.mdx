Hemp seed oil (Cannabis Sativa seed oil) is a plant-derived oil pressed from hemp seeds that delivers linoleic acid and gamma-linolenic acid directly to the skin barrier. Skin affected by dermatitis and eczema typically shows a disrupted lipid barrier, and these fatty acids replenish the lipid layer to restore normal barrier function. Hemp seed oil's fatty acid profile mirrors the skin's own intercellular lipids, so the oil absorbs without leaving a heavy residue.

Era Organics Dermatitis Cream includes hemp seed oil specifically for its barrier-restoring fatty acid composition. Linoleic acid is the predominant fatty acid in healthy outer skin layer (stratum corneum) lipids, and barrier-compromised skin loses it at an accelerated rate. Hemp seed oil reintroduces linoleic acid topically, reducing moisture loss through the skin (transepidermal water loss) and softening the rough, flaky texture associated with chronic dry skin and eczema. A systematic review and meta-analysis of complementary and alternative medicine treatments found a standard mean difference of -0.70 (95% CI -1.19, -0.21) in the eczema area and severity index score, supporting the relevance of plant-derived ingredients in eczema management (Jones et al., JAAD International, 2021).

The Relief OTC Cream pairs hemp seed oil with colloidal oatmeal 1%, the active skin protectant, so the formula addresses barrier restoration and surface-level skin protection at the same time. Colloidal oatmeal forms a protective film over inflamed skin while hemp seed oil works beneath the surface to reinforce the lipid matrix. Era Organics Relief rounds out this pairing with glycerin, shea butter, and hyaluronic acid to deliver layered moisture retention alongside the fatty acid replenishment hemp seed oil provides.

Patch test before first use, particularly on skin with a known sensitivity to Cannabis Sativa botanicals.
